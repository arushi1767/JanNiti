# Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)
ministry: Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare
apply: https://pmfby.gov.in
official: https://pmfby.gov.in
mode: online
aliases: pmfby | fasal bima | crop insurance | pradhan mantri fasal bima yojana

## What is this scheme
Launched on 18 February 2016, PMFBY is a crop insurance scheme by the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers' Welfare. It provides financial protection to farmers against crop loss due to natural disasters (hail, drought, famine), pests and diseases, at a cost-effective premium, implemented through a network of insurance companies and banks. It covers over 50 crore farmers and 50+ crops.

## Why it was introduced
- To financially support farmers suffering crop damage from unforeseen events.
- To stabilise farmers' income and ensure continuance in farming.
- To encourage modern and innovative agricultural practices.
- To ensure crop diversification and credit-worthiness of farmers.

## Benefits
- Affordable premium: farmer pays max 2% for Kharif food & oilseed crops, 1.5% for Rabi food & oilseeds, 5% for annual commercial/horticultural crops — government subsidises the rest.
- In North-Eastern States, Jammu & Kashmir and Himachal Pradesh, the government pays the entire premium.
- Covers natural disasters, pests, diseases; post-harvest losses (up to 14 days, cut-and-spread condition) and localised calamities (hailstorm, landslide, inundation) on an individual farm basis.
- Prevented sowing cover: indemnity up to 25% of sum insured if weather prevents sowing.
- Claims targeted within two months of harvest; satellite imaging, drones and mobile apps used for accurate loss estimation.

## Eligibility
- All farmers, including tenant farmers and sharecroppers, growing notified crops in notified areas.
- Farmer must have an insurable interest in the crop and a valid land ownership certificate or valid land tenure agreement.
- Must apply within the prescribed window — usually within 2 weeks of the start of the sowing season.
- Must not have received compensation for the same crop loss from another source.

## Exclusions
- Crop losses in non-notified areas are generally not covered.
- Losses outside the specified crop cycle.
- Losses from farmer negligence or non-compliance with recommended practices.
- Losses beyond specified limits/thresholds.
- Non-payment of premium cancels coverage.

## Required Documents
- Passport-size photograph.
- Bank passbook.
- Identity proof (Aadhaar / PAN / Voter ID / bank passbook with photo / Kisan Photo Book / NREGA job card).
- Address proof (Aadhaar / PAN / Voter ID / electricity bill / bank passbook with photo).
- Land records evidence — State Records of Right (RoR), Land Possession Certificate (LPC) or other state-permitted agreements/contracts.
- Declaration about the crop sown / intended to be sown.

## How to Apply
1. Visit pmfby.gov.in and click "Farmer Corner"; choose "Guest Farmer" to register (skip if registered).
2. Fill Farmer Details, Residential Details, Farmer ID and Account Details sections; enter captcha; "Create User".
3. Log in with your mobile number + OTP at the Farmer Login page.
4. Fill the application form, upload mandatory documents in the specified format/size, preview and submit.
5. Choose "Make Payment" (or "Pay Later") and keep the receipt. Track status via "Application Status" on the homepage.
Offline and CSC application is also available.

## Important Dates
- Apply within about 2 weeks of the start of each sowing season (exact cut-off dates are notified per state/season on pmfby.gov.in).

## Hidden Conditions & Rules
- Missing the seasonal enrolment cut-off means no cover for that crop season. [red]
- Only notified crops in notified areas are covered — check your district's notification. [red]
- Losses due to negligence or not following recommended practices can be rejected. [yellow]
- Post-harvest cover lasts only 14 days and only for crops in cut-and-spread condition. [yellow]
