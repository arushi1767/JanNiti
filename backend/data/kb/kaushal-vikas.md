## What is this scheme
The Short Term Training (STT) component at PMKVY Training Centres benefits Indian nationals who are school/college dropouts or unemployed. Beyond NSQF-aligned training, centres teach soft skills, entrepreneurship, financial and digital literacy. After assessment, training providers give placement assistance. STT covers fresh skilling for first-time learners and re-skilling for the existing workforce; duration varies by job role. English, Employability & Entrepreneurship (EEE) modules and bridge/language courses raise international employability.

## Why it was introduced
To enable large numbers of Indian youth to take industry-relevant skill training, get certified and secure a better livelihood.

## Benefits
- Counselling: online platform, helpline and district-level skill information centres.
- Training: digital content; soft skills, entrepreneurship, financial and digital literacy.
- Additional support: accidental insurance; one-time incentive to certified candidates; boarding & lodging support; conveyance cost; post-placement stipend; extra support for PwD candidates; induction kit and handbook.
- Placement support: one-time placement travel cost, career progression support, special incentive for foreign placements, post-placement tracking.

## Eligibility
- Indian national aged 15-45 years.
- Possesses an Aadhaar card and an Aadhaar-linked bank account.
- Fulfils other criteria for the respective job role as defined by the awarding body.

## Required Documents
- As required for the chosen job role (Aadhaar is mandatory at enrolment).

## How to Apply
- Contact your nearest Training Centre to apply. Find centres at: https://www.pmkvyofficial.org/trainingcenter
- Candidate registration is also available through the Skill India Digital portal (skillindiadigital.gov.in).

## Important Dates
- Training batches run through the year at centres; ask your nearest centre for the next batch.

## Hidden Conditions & Rules
- Aadhaar is mandatory at enrolment. [red]
- Minimum 70% attendance is mandatory to appear in assessments. [red]
- Higher-level courses (NSQF 5+) may be fee-based; weaker/marginalised sections continue to be supported. [yellow]
