# Skill Loan Scheme (Model Skill Loan Scheme)
ministry: Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship
apply: https://www.jansamarth.in

aliases: skill loan | model skill loan | skilling loan | vidya kaushal

## What is this scheme
Launched in July 2015, the Skill Loan Scheme provides institutional credit for skill development courses aligned to National Occupational Standards and Qualification Packs, leading to a certificate/diploma/degree under the National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF). It applies to all IBA member banks and other RBI-advised institutions.

## Why it was introduced
So that no student who has secured admission in a recognised skill course is denied training for lack of money.

## Benefits
- Loan amount: Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 1,50,000.
- Courses: NSQF-aligned; no minimum course duration.
- Interest: base rate (MCLR) + typically up to 1.5%.
- Moratorium: duration of the course.
- Repayment: 3-7 years by size — up to Rs. 50,000: 3 years; Rs. 50,000-1 lakh: 5 years; above Rs. 1 lakh: 7 years.
- Covers course fees (paid directly to the institute) plus assessment, examination and study material expenses.
- NO collateral can be charged from the beneficiary; loans are backed by the Credit Guarantee Fund for Skill Development (CGFSSD) via NCGTC (guarantee up to 75% of outstanding, fee <= 0.5%).

## Eligibility
- Any individual who has secured admission in a course run by ITIs, Polytechnics, schools recognised by Central/State boards, colleges affiliated to recognised universities, or training partners affiliated to NSDC / Sector Skill Councils / State Skill Missions / State Skill Corporations.

## Required Documents
- Proof of identity.
- Proof of address.
- Proof of income (of self or guardian, if available).
- Admission proof of the NSQF-aligned course (as required by the bank).

## How to Apply
1. Register on the Vidya Kaushal portal (or apply via the Jan Samarth portal / your bank).
2. Select your preferred sector / role / centre after registration.
3. Visit the chosen centre for counselling.
4. Raise a loan request through the centre if needed.
5. Evaluate and accept/reject loan offers.
6. Loan is disbursed directly to the training partner/centre after confirmation.

## Important Dates
- No deadline; apply after securing admission to a recognised course.

## Hidden Conditions & Rules
- The bank pays the institute directly — you don't receive the fee money in hand. [green]
- No collateral can be demanded — if a bank asks, that violates the scheme. [yellow]
- Interest accrues during the course moratorium even though repayment starts later. [yellow]
