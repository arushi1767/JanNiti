## What is this scheme
The UGC's JRF in Engineering & Technology is for candidates pursuing research leading to a Ph.D. in engineering and technology (including agricultural engineering). Since UGC/CSIR do not conduct NET in these fields, M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma students are selected through direct interviews by the Commission. There are 50 slots per year. Subjects like geology and geophysics are not covered.

## Why it was introduced
To give research scholars an opportunity for advanced study and research in engineering and technology leading to a Ph.D.

## Benefits
- JRF tenure initially 2 years; on satisfactory evaluation, extended 3 more years as SRF with enhanced emoluments (total 5 years, non-extendable). Unsatisfactory work gets 1 improvement year as JRF, then re-evaluation.
- Fellowship: Rs. 14,000/month for the first two years; Rs. 15,000/month thereafter.
- Contingency: Rs. 12,000/year (first two years); Rs. 25,000/year thereafter.
- Departmental assistance: Rs. 3,000/year per student to the host institute.
- Escort/Reader assistance Rs. 2,000/month for physically handicapped and blind candidates.
- HRA per host institution rules (or hostel accommodation; hostel fee reimbursable excluding mess/electricity/water).
- Leave: max 30 days/year plus public holidays; women get 135 days' maternity leave at full fellowship once during tenure.

## Eligibility
- Master's degree in engineering/technology/pharmacy with 55% marks (M.E./M.Tech. degree required).
- GATE is NOT mandatory.
- Age limit 40 years as on 1 July of the award year; +5 years relaxation for women and SC/ST.
- 22.5% of fellowships reserved for SC/ST (released to general if unavailable).

## Exclusions (cancellation of award)
- Misconduct; unsatisfactory research progress; failure in any Ph.D.-related examination; candidate found ineligible later.

## Required Documents
- Duly filled application form.
- Aadhaar number.
- Proof of DOB (birth certificate or class 10 mark sheet).
- Proof of address.
- Educational qualification proofs (mark sheets, certificates, diplomas).
- Certificate from the head of department/institution.
- Research abstract (max 500 words) on proposed work and methods.
- Employment status proof (if employed).
- Declaration; Basic Data Sheet.
- Caste / minority / disability certificate (if applicable).
- Bank details; government-issued ID proof.

## How to Apply
- Applications are invited once a year in the prescribed format (Annexure II) through advertisements in leading newspapers and the AIU Newsletter.
- Shortlisted candidates are interviewed by a UGC expert committee (no TA/DA for the interview). Selection is by merit; the Commission's decision is final.
- After selection, the Joining Report signed by supervisor/HoD goes to UGC via registrar/principal; the first-year grant is then released.

## Important Dates
- Watch UGC advertisements (newspapers / AIU Newsletter) once a year for the application window.

## Hidden Conditions & Rules
- Fellowship can be cancelled for misconduct, poor progress or exam failure. [red]
- Only 50 slots per year nationally — highly competitive. [yellow]
- Leave without fellowship counts toward the 5-year total tenure. [yellow]
- Teaching assignments allowed only in special cases, up to one academic year, in the same city. [green]
