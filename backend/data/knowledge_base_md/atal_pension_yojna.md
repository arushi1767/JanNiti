# Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

## Overview
Atal Pension Yojana lets workers aged 18-40 in the unorganised sector pay a small monthly amount now to get a Government-guaranteed pension of Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 per month from age 60 for life.

In plain terms: You choose a pension slab (Rs. 1,000-5,000), pay a small fixed contribution till 60, and the Government guarantees that pension for you, and then for your spouse. Atal Pension Yojana (APY) (APY) is a central sector voluntary pension scheme (Government-guaranteed pension) scheme of Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. The Union Cabinet approved continuation of APY up to FY 2030-31 (on 21 January 2026); APY crossed about 9 crore gross enrolments by April 2026.

**Primary sources for this section:** PFRDA (official FAQ); Ministry of Finance / DFS; PIB; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
Atal Pension Yojana lets workers aged 18-40 in the unorganised sector pay a small monthly amount now to get a Government-guaranteed pension of Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 per month from age 60 for life.

## Storytelling Version
A vegetable vendor or domestic worker usually has no pension waiting for them in old age. Atal Pension Yojana fixes that: by saving a small amount every month from a young age — sometimes the price of a cup of tea a day — you lock in a guaranteed monthly pension of Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 after you turn 60, for the rest of your life. After you, your spouse keeps getting the same pension, and finally the saved corpus goes to your nominee. The earlier you join, the less you pay each month.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Most workers in the unorganised sector have no formal pension and risk financial hardship in old age. APY was launched in 2015 to give them an affordable, Government-guaranteed pension, encouraging voluntary retirement savings and reducing old-age dependence. It is regulated by PFRDA under the National Pension System framework. _Source: PFRDA; Ministry of Finance; PIB._

## Key Benefits
- Government-guaranteed monthly pension of Rs. 1,000, Rs. 2,000, Rs. 3,000, Rs. 4,000 or Rs. 5,000 from age 60, for life.
- Same pension continues to the spouse after the subscriber's death.
- On death of both subscriber and spouse, the accumulated pension corpus goes to the nominee.
- Low, age-based contributions (the younger you start, the less you pay) via auto-debit — monthly, quarterly or half-yearly.
- Tax benefits on contributions under Sections 80CCD(1) and the additional Rs. 50,000 under 80CCD(1B).
- Sovereign guarantee on the minimum pension.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for APY:
- Any Indian citizen aged 18 to 40 years.
- Must have an active savings bank account or post office savings account.
- Aadhaar and a mobile number must be linked to the account.
- Must NOT be (and must not have been) an income-tax payer on or after 1 October 2022.
- NRIs and OCI cardholders are not eligible.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Income-tax payers cannot join (rule effective 1 October 2022); if found to be a taxpayer at the time of joining, the account is closed and only accumulated savings are returned.
- Contributions are auto-debited from the bank account — keep enough balance, or penalties (Rs. 1-Rs. 10/month) apply and the account can be deactivated/closed on long default.
- From 1 October 2025, enrolment is only on the revised APY form (with FATCA/CRS declaration).
- The pension slab is chosen at joining; it can be changed only once a year through the official process.
- Government co-contribution applied only to those who joined between 1 June 2015 and 31 March 2016 (and met conditions); it ended on 31 March 2020.
- Premature exit before 60 is allowed only in cases like terminal illness or death; otherwise only contributions plus net returns (less charges) are refunded.

## Required Documents
- Active savings bank/post office account (KYC fetched from it).
- Aadhaar (for KYC/identity).
- Aadhaar-linked mobile number.
- Nominee details (mandatory).
- Revised APY subscriber registration form with FATCA/CRS declaration (from 1 October 2025).

## Application Process
**Through your bank/post office:** Submit the APY registration form, choose pension slab and contribution frequency, give consent for auto-debit, and provide nominee details. KYC is taken from your existing savings account.

**Online:** Log in to your net banking and search 'APY', or use the e-APY portal (enps.nps-proteantech.in) -> 'Atal Pension Yojana' -> 'APY Registration', complete KYC via Aadhaar OTP, fill personal and nominee details, choose the pension amount and frequency, and e-sign. A PRAN (Permanent Retirement Account Number) is generated. _Source: PFRDA._

## Processing Time
Account is usually activated within a few working days of submitting the form and first auto-debit; PRAN is generated on successful registration.

## Verification Process
The bank/post office verifies KYC from the linked savings account and Aadhaar; PFRDA's Central Recordkeeping Agency maintains the records and issues the PRAN.

## Approval Criteria
Enrolment is approved for any eligible 18-40 year-old, non-taxpayer Indian citizen with a savings account and complete KYC/nominee details.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Age outside 18-40 at joining.
- Applicant is/was an income-tax payer (on or after 1 Oct 2022).
- No active savings/post office account or incomplete KYC.
- Missing or incomplete nominee details.
- NRI/OCI status.

## Renewal Process
No renewal needed. Contributions continue by auto-debit until age 60; keep the account funded to avoid penalties and deactivation.

## Cancellation Conditions
The account is closed if the subscriber is found to be an ineligible taxpayer (savings returned), on voluntary exit (contributions plus net returns refunded), or on prolonged default. Premature exit before 60 is restricted to specified cases.

## State-wise Variations
Uniform across India — same slabs, contributions and guarantee everywhere; available through all banks and post offices.

## Latest Updates
- Cabinet approved continuation of APY up to FY 2030-31 on 21 January 2026.
- APY crossed about 9 crore gross enrolments by 21 April 2026; FY 2025-26 saw the highest-ever new additions (~1.35 crore).
- From 1 October 2025, only the revised APY form (with FATCA/CRS declaration) is accepted.
- Income-tax payers barred from joining since 1 October 2022.
- PFRDA describes APY as a 'Sampurna Suraksha Kavach' (complete security shield).

## Application Deadline
Currently Open. You can join any time while aged 18-40. There is no annual deadline, but the earlier you join the lower your monthly contribution.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much pension will I get under APY?**

A guaranteed Rs. 1,000, Rs. 2,000, Rs. 3,000, Rs. 4,000 or Rs. 5,000 per month from age 60 for life, depending on the slab you choose and your contributions.

**Q: Who can join Atal Pension Yojana?**

Any Indian citizen aged 18 to 40 with a savings bank or post office account, who is not (and has not been) an income-tax payer on or after 1 October 2022.

**Q: Can income-tax payers join APY?**

No. Since 1 October 2022, taxpayers are not eligible. If someone joins and is later found to have been a taxpayer at the time of joining, the account is closed and the accumulated amount is returned.

**Q: What happens to my APY after I die?**

Your spouse continues to receive the same pension for life. After both of you, the accumulated corpus is paid to your nominee.

**Q: How much do I have to contribute each month?**

It depends on your joining age and chosen pension. The younger you start, the less you pay (for Rs. 5,000 pension, roughly Rs. 210/month at age 18 rising with age). See the official APY contribution chart.

**Q: What if I miss a contribution?**

A small penalty of about Rs. 1 to Rs. 10 per month applies until dues are cleared; prolonged default can freeze or close the account.

**Q: Can I get tax benefits on APY?**

Yes — contributions qualify under Section 80CCD(1) and the additional Rs. 50,000 deduction under 80CCD(1B) (old tax regime).

**Q: Has APY been extended?**

Yes. The Cabinet approved its continuation up to FY 2030-31 on 21 January 2026.

**Q: Can I change my pension amount later?**

Yes, the pension slab can be increased or decreased once a year through the official process.

**Q: Is APY a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Atal Pension Yojana (APY) is a Central Sector voluntary pension scheme (Government-guaranteed pension) scheme administered by Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for APY?**

Applying for Atal Pension Yojana (APY) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://www.pfrda.org.in.

**Q: Which is the official website for APY?**

The official website is https://www.pfrda.org.in. The official application portal is https://enps.nps-proteantech.in / e-APY / through banks & post offices. Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

**Q: What is the helpline number for APY?**

The official helpline / contact for Atal Pension Yojana (APY) is: APY Toll-Free 1800-110-069. Use only official helplines listed on https://www.pfrda.org.in; never share OTPs or bank passwords with callers.

**Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for APY?**

Aadhaar (and an Aadhaar-linked mobile) must be linked to your savings account for APY enrolment and KYC.

**Q: Can I apply for APY at a Common Service Centre (CSC)?**

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

**Q: How will I receive the benefit under APY?**

APY is a contributory pension — you pay in via auto-debit; the guaranteed pension is later paid to your bank account.

**Q: What should I do if my APY application is rejected?**

Check the rejection reason on the official portal or with the office/bank that processed it, correct the specific issue (commonly an Aadhaar/name/bank mismatch or a missing document), and re-apply or file a grievance through the official grievance channel. Most rejections are due to fixable data errors, not permanent disqualification.

**Q: Is APY still active in 2026?**

Yes. APY is active and has been extended up to FY 2030-31 (Cabinet approval on 21 January 2026).

**Q: Can I benefit from APY along with other government schemes?**

It depends on the specific scheme rules. Some benefits can be combined with other schemes; others have exclusions. Check the eligibility and hidden-conditions sections, or confirm with the implementing office/bank before assuming you can hold both.

**Q: How can I check my application or beneficiary status for APY?**

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**APY vs NPS:** APY gives a guaranteed fixed pension (low-risk); NPS is market-linked with no guarantee but potentially higher returns. **APY vs PMJJBY/PMSBY:** all three are 'Jan Suraksha' schemes — APY is pension, PMJJBY is life insurance, PMSBY is accident insurance; many people hold all three.

## Who Should Apply
Young workers in the unorganised sector (18-40) — vendors, drivers, domestic workers, daily-wage and self-employed people — who want a guaranteed pension and are not taxpayers.

## Who Should Not Apply
Income-tax payers, NRIs/OCIs, people over 40, and those who prefer market-linked higher-return products (consider NPS).

## Decision Helper
- **Young unorganised-sector worker:** Join early for the lowest contribution and a guaranteed pension.
- **Homemaker (non-taxpayer):** Eligible — open an APY in your own name.
- **Income-tax payer:** Not eligible; consider NPS.
- **Want higher (market) returns and can take risk:** Consider NPS instead.
