# CBSE Merit Scholarship Scheme for Single Girl Child (SGC)

## Overview
The CBSE SGC scholarship gives Rs. 1,000 per month for two years to single girl children who scored 70%+ in CBSE Class 10 and are continuing in Class 11 and 12 in a CBSE-affiliated school.

In plain terms: A single girl child who did well in Class 10 (70%+) gets Rs. 1,000 a month through Classes 11 and 12 to continue her studies. CBSE Merit Scholarship Scheme for Single Girl Child (SGC) (CBSE SGC Scholarship) is a central scholarship scheme (merit-cum-category) scheme of Ministry of Education. Status as of 2026: Active (annual). Provides Rs. 1,000 per month for up to two years to meritorious single girl children studying in Classes 11 and 12 in CBSE schools.

**Primary sources for this section:** CBSE (cbse.gov.in, official); Ministry of Education; scheme PDF.

## One Line Summary
The CBSE SGC scholarship gives Rs. 1,000 per month for two years to single girl children who scored 70%+ in CBSE Class 10 and are continuing in Class 11 and 12 in a CBSE-affiliated school.

## Storytelling Version
This scholarship is the Government's way of saying every daughter's education matters. If a family has a single girl child who is a CBSE student and scored 70% or more in Class 10, she gets Rs. 1,000 every month for two years while she studies in Class 11 and 12. It rewards merit, encourages families to invest in their daughters, and helps meritorious girls keep studying without dropping out.

## Why This Scheme Exists
To promote education among single girl children and encourage families to value and continue their daughters' schooling, CBSE (under the Ministry of Education) provides financial assistance to meritorious single girls in senior secondary classes. It recognises academic excellence in Class 10 and supports continuation through Class 12. _Source: CBSE; Ministry of Education._

## Key Benefits
- Financial assistance of Rs. 1,000 per month per student.
- Paid for a maximum of two years (Classes 11 and 12).
- Disbursed by ECS/NEFT (electronic transfer) to the student's account.
- Renewable for the second year subject to conditions (continued study, conduct, attendance).

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for CBSE SGC Scholarship:
- Applicant must be a single girl child of her parents (no siblings); twins/all children born together count as a single girl child.
- Secured 70% or more marks in the CBSE Class 10 examination.
- Studying in Class 11 or 12 in a CBSE-affiliated school.
- Tuition fee not exceeding Rs. 2,500 per month in Class 10, and not exceeding Rs. 3,000 per month in Classes 11 and 12.
- Gross parental/family income not exceeding Rs. 8,00,000 per annum.
- Indian national; must continue studies in Classes 11 and 12.
- NRI students are eligible with tuition fee not exceeding Rs. 6,000 per month.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- There are tuition-fee CEILINGS — exceeding the monthly fee limit (Rs. 2,500 in Class 10; Rs. 3,000 in Class 11-12; Rs. 6,000 for NRIs) makes the girl ineligible.
- Family income must not exceed Rs. 8 lakh per annum.
- The application must be VERIFIED by the school.
- The student must maintain good conduct and regular attendance to retain/renew the scholarship.
- Renewal (for Class 12) requires re-application and submission of documents within the cycle.
- An income affidavit on stamp paper and a Gazetted-officer/Magistrate-attested affidavit are required.

## Required Documents
- Passport-size photograph and scanned signature.
- Aadhaar card.
- Admission proof and student's ID card.
- Class 10 mark sheet (and Class 11 mark sheet for renewal cases).
- Bank account details (Aadhaar-linked).
- Fee receipt verified by the school principal.
- Income affidavit on Rs. 50 stamp paper (self-declaration by parent), attested by a Gazetted Officer / SDM / First-Class Magistrate.

## Application Process
**Online:** On the CBSE official website (cbse.gov.in) scholarship section, click 'New User Registration' (or 'Renewal'), read the instructions and continue; enter your roll number and date of birth as per the Class 10 mark sheet.

**Affidavit & undertaking:** Print the undertaking from the Guidelines, fill it, paste your photo, get it attested by the school, and prepare the income affidavit. Scan both as PDFs (each under 1 MB, all pages) and upload under 'Upload Document'. Print the confirmation page.

**Renewal:** Courier the duly filled, signed confirmation page to the CBSE Scholarship Cell as instructed. The school must verify the application. _Source: CBSE._

## Processing Time
Processed in the annual scholarship cycle after school verification; disbursement is by ECS/NEFT once approved.

## Verification Process
The school verifies the application, fee receipt and the student's eligibility/conduct; CBSE processes verified applications.

## Approval Criteria
Approved for single girl children meeting the 70% marks, fee-ceiling, income and CBSE-school conditions, with a school-verified application.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Not a single girl child / has siblings (except twins counted as single).
- Class 10 marks below 70%.
- Tuition fee above the prescribed ceiling, or family income above Rs. 8 lakh.
- Application not verified by the school / incomplete affidavits or documents.
- PDF documents not meeting format/size (under 1 MB, all pages).

## Renewal Process
Renewable for the second year (Class 12) by re-applying as a 'Renewal' case, with the Class 11 mark sheet, continued eligibility, conduct and attendance.

## Cancellation Conditions
Scholarship stops if the student discontinues, fails to maintain conduct/attendance, or is found ineligible (e.g. fee/income limits breached, false information).

## State-wise Variations
Uniform across CBSE-affiliated schools nationwide; not State-specific.

## Latest Updates

- Applications (new and renewal) are filed online on the CBSE website each cycle, usually after Class 10 results.
- Tuition-fee ceilings and the Rs. 8 lakh family-income limit apply.

## Application Deadline
Annual cycle. The application window typically opens after the CBSE Class 10 results are declared; check cbse.gov.in for the current year's exact dates.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much does the CBSE Single Girl Child scholarship pay?**

Rs. 1,000 per month per student, for a maximum of two years (Classes 11 and 12), transferred electronically (ECS/NEFT).

**Q: Who is a 'single girl child' for this scheme?**

A girl who is the only child of her parents (no siblings). Twins/children born together are treated as a single girl child.

**Q: What marks do I need in Class 10?**

70% or more in the CBSE Class 10 examination, while continuing in Class 11/12 in a CBSE-affiliated school.

**Q: Is there a family income or fee limit?**

Yes. Family income must not exceed Rs. 8 lakh per year; tuition fee must not exceed Rs. 2,500/month in Class 10 and Rs. 3,000/month in Classes 11-12 (Rs. 6,000/month for NRIs).

**Q: Can NRI students apply?**

Yes, if the tuition fee does not exceed Rs. 6,000 per month and other conditions are met.

**Q: How do I apply?**

Online on cbse.gov.in (scholarship section): register, enter your Class 10 roll number and DOB, upload the attested undertaking and income affidavit as PDFs (each under 1 MB), and submit; the school must verify the application.

**Q: How do I renew it for Class 12?**

Re-apply as a 'Renewal' case with your Class 11 mark sheet and continued eligibility/conduct; courier the signed confirmation page to CBSE as instructed.

**Q: Which documents are mandatory?**

Aadhaar, Class 10 mark sheet, school-verified fee receipt, an income affidavit on Rs. 50 stamp paper attested by a Gazetted Officer/SDM/First-Class Magistrate, bank details, and a photo/signature.
