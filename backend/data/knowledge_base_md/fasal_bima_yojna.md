# Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

## Overview
PMFBY gives farmers affordable crop insurance — pay just 2% (Kharif), 1.5% (Rabi) or 5% (commercial/horticultural) of the sum insured, and the Government subsidises the rest, so crop loss from natural calamities, pests and disease is compensated.

In plain terms: A farmer pays a small fixed share of the premium and is protected against crop loss from drought, flood, hailstorm, pests, disease and other notified risks. Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) (PMFBY) is a central sector crop insurance scheme (premium subsidy shared with states) scheme of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. Status as of 2026: Active and voluntary for all farmers since 2020; the scheme has been continued/renewed for 2025-26 with technology-led claim assessment (YES-TECH, WINDS, AIDE/Saral app).

## One Line Summary
PMFBY gives farmers affordable crop insurance — pay just 2% (Kharif), 1.5% (Rabi) or 5% (commercial/horticultural) of the sum insured, and the Government subsidises the rest, so crop loss from natural calamities, pests and disease is compensated.

## Storytelling Version
Farming is a gamble against the weather — one hailstorm or a failed monsoon can wipe out a season's income. PMFBY lets a farmer insure the crop for a tiny premium: about 2% of the insured value for Kharif crops, 1.5% for Rabi, and 5% for commercial/horticultural crops. The Government pays the much larger remaining premium. If the notified crop fails due to a covered cause, the farmer gets a claim payout, so one bad season does not push the family into debt.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Crop loss from drought, flood, pests and disease can ruin small farmers and trap them in debt. PMFBY was launched in 2016 to provide comprehensive, affordable, technology-driven crop insurance with a uniform low farmer premium, stabilising farm income and encouraging modern practices. The Government subsidises most of the premium and uses satellite imaging, drones and mobile apps to assess losses accurately. _Source: Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare; pmfby.gov.in._

## Key Benefits
- Low farmer premium: 2% of sum insured for Kharif food/oilseed crops, 1.5% for Rabi, and 5% for annual commercial/horticultural crops — Government subsidises the rest.
- For farmers in North-Eastern States and certain regions, the Government may bear the entire premium share.
- Comprehensive cover: natural fire/lightning, storm, hailstorm, cyclone, flood, inundation, landslide, drought, pests and diseases.
- Prevented-sowing cover: indemnity up to 25% of sum insured when sowing is prevented by adverse weather.
- Post-harvest losses: cover up to 14 days for crops kept in 'cut and spread' condition, against cyclone/unseasonal rain.
- Localised calamities: individual-farm assessment for hailstorm, landslide, inundation.
- Technology-driven, faster claims (satellite, drones, mobile apps); aim to settle within about two months of harvest.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PMFBY:
- All farmers growing notified crops in notified areas, including sharecroppers and tenant farmers.
- Farmer must have an insurable interest in the insured crop.
- Valid land ownership document or land tenure/tenancy agreement.
- Application must be filed within the prescribed cut-off date for the season.
- Farmer must not have claimed compensation for the same loss from another source.
- Voluntary for all farmers (including loanee farmers) since 2020.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Cover applies only to NOTIFIED crops in NOTIFIED areas — crops/areas not notified are not covered.
- Application must reach the insurer before the season's cut-off date.
- Losses outside the defined crop cycle, or from farmer negligence/non-compliance with recommended practices, may be excluded.
- Non-payment of the farmer's premium share voids cover.
- Since 2020 the scheme is voluntary even for KCC/loanee farmers, who must opt out in writing if they do not want it (else it continues).
- Double compensation for the same loss from another source is not allowed.

## Required Documents
- Passport-size photograph.
- Bank passbook (for premium debit and claim credit).
- Identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID/Bank passbook with photo/Kisan photo book/NREGA job card).
- Address proof.
- Land records / Record of Rights (RoR), Land Possession Certificate (LPC), or tenancy agreement.
- Sowing declaration (crop sown / intended to be sown).

## Application Process
**Online:** Visit https://pmfby.gov.in -> 'Farmer Corner' -> 'Guest Farmer' to register, then log in (mobile + OTP) and fill the Application Form, upload documents, preview, submit, and pay the premium (or 'Pay Later').

**Through bank/CSC:** Loanee and non-loanee farmers can enrol through their bank branch or a Common Service Centre before the cut-off date.

**Report a loss:** Intimate crop loss within 72 hours through the AIDE/Crop Insurance app, the toll-free helpline 14447, the insurer, bank, or agriculture office. Track status via 'Application Status' on the portal. _Source: pmfby.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Claims are targeted for settlement within about two months of harvest; loss intimation for localised/post-harvest events must be within 72 hours of the event.

## Verification Process
Losses are assessed using Crop Cutting Experiments (CCE), satellite/remote sensing (YES-TECH), weather data (WINDS) and field/drone surveys; individual-farm losses (localised/post-harvest) are inspected on the ground.

## Approval Criteria
A claim is approved when the insured notified crop suffers a covered loss within the policy period, the premium was paid, loss was intimated in time, and assessment confirms the loss.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Crop or area not notified for the season.
- Premium not paid / application after cut-off date.
- Loss intimation not given within 72 hours (for applicable risks).
- Loss due to excluded causes (negligence, war, theft, etc.).
- Already compensated for the same loss elsewhere.

## Renewal Process
Insurance is per season — farmers re-enrol each Kharif/Rabi season before the cut-off. Loanee farmers continue automatically unless they opt out in writing.

## Cancellation Conditions
Cover lapses if the premium is not debited/paid; a farmer may opt out (non-loanee, or loanee with written request) before the cut-off date.

## State-wise Variations
States notify which crops and areas are covered each season, set cut-off dates and pick insurers; some States have opted out or run their own crop-insurance schemes, so availability and crops vary by State and season.

## Latest Updates
- Voluntary for all farmers since Kharif 2020.
- Technology rollout: YES-TECH (satellite yield estimation), WINDS (weather stations), and the AIDE/Saral Fasal Bima app for assisted enrolment and digital loss intimation.
- Scheme continued for 2025-26; some States have rejoined while a few remain outside the scheme.
- Crop Insurance mobile app and helpline 14447 for enrolment, loss reporting and claim tracking.

## Application Deadline
Season-based. For Kharif, the cut-off is typically around 31 July; for Rabi, around 31 December. Exact dates are notified by each State every season — check pmfby.gov.in before sowing.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much premium do I pay under PMFBY?**

Just 2% of the sum insured for Kharif food/oilseed crops, 1.5% for Rabi, and 5% for annual commercial/horticultural crops. The Government subsidises the much larger balance.

**Q: Is PMFBY compulsory if I have a crop loan (KCC)?**

No. Since 2020 PMFBY is voluntary for everyone, including loanee/KCC farmers. If you do not want it you must inform your bank in writing before the cut-off date, otherwise it continues automatically.

**Q: What crop losses are covered?**

Natural fire/lightning, storm, hailstorm, cyclone, flood, inundation, landslide, drought, pests and diseases; plus prevented sowing, post-harvest losses (up to 14 days), and localised calamities.

**Q: How and when do I report a crop loss?**

Within 72 hours of the event, through the Crop Insurance/AIDE app, helpline 14447, your insurer, bank, or agriculture office.

**Q: What is the cut-off date to enrol?**

It is season- and State-specific — typically around 31 July for Kharif and 31 December for Rabi. Always check the exact notified date on pmfby.gov.in for your State.

**Q: Are tenant and sharecropper farmers covered?**

Yes, if they grow a notified crop in a notified area and have an insurable interest with a valid tenancy/land document.

**Q: When will I get my claim?**

PMFBY aims to settle claims within about two months of harvest, using satellite, weather and field assessments.

**Q: What is not covered by PMFBY?**

Crops/areas not notified, losses outside the crop cycle, losses from negligence or excluded causes, and any loss already compensated from another source.

**Q: Is PMFBY a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a Central Sector crop insurance scheme (premium subsidy shared with States) scheme administered by Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for PMFBY?**

Aadhaar is used as identity proof for enrolment and to credit claims to your bank account; keep Aadhaar and bank details linked.

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

Claim amounts are paid directly to the insured farmer's bank account through DBT.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**PMFBY vs Restructured Weather Based Crop Insurance (RWBCIS):** PMFBY is yield-based; RWBCIS pays on weather indices. **PMFBY vs KCC/PM-KISAN:** PMFBY is insurance (risk cover), KCC is credit, PM-KISAN is income support — farmers often use all three. Some States run their own crop-insurance schemes as alternatives.

## Who Should Apply
Farmers growing notified crops in notified areas, especially in drought/flood/hail-prone regions, who want low-cost protection against crop loss.

## Who Should Not Apply
Farmers whose crop/area is not notified, or who already have equivalent crop cover and do not want overlapping insurance.

## Decision Helper
- **Farmer in a risk-prone area:** Enrol before the season cut-off — premium is only 1.5-5%.
- **KCC/loanee farmer:** You are auto-covered unless you opt out in writing.
- **Crop/area not notified:** PMFBY won't cover it; check State alternatives.
- **Want income support, not insurance:** See PM-KISAN.
