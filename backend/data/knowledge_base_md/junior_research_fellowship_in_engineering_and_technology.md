# Junior Research Fellowship in Engineering & Technology (UGC)

## Overview
The UGC Junior Research Fellowship in Engineering & Technology lets M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma graduates pursue a Ph.D. with a fellowship — 2 years as JRF, then 3 years as SRF on satisfactory progress.

In plain terms: An M.Tech/M.E./M.Pharma graduate doing a Ph.D. in engineering/technology gets a UGC fellowship for up to 5 years (2 as JRF, 3 as SRF). Junior Research Fellowship in Engineering & Technology (UGC) (UGC JRF (Engineering & Technology)) is a central research fellowship (Ph.D. in engineering & technology) scheme of Ministry of Education. Status as of 2026: Active. A UGC fellowship (about 50 slots per year) enabling M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma graduates to pursue Ph.D. research in engineering and technology, with 2 years JRF then 3 years SRF.

## One Line Summary
The UGC Junior Research Fellowship in Engineering & Technology lets M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma graduates pursue a Ph.D. with a fellowship — 2 years as JRF, then 3 years as SRF on satisfactory progress.

## Storytelling Version
There is no national NET exam in engineering and technology the way there is in other subjects, so the UGC created this Junior Research Fellowship to support engineers who want to do a Ph.D. M.E., M.Tech. and M.Pharma graduates are selected through direct interviews by the Commission (about 50 slots a year). You start as a Junior Research Fellow for two years; if your work is found satisfactory, you continue for three more years as a Senior Research Fellow with higher emoluments — supporting up to five years of advanced research toward your Ph.D.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Because UGC/CSIR do not conduct the NET examination in engineering and technology, talented M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma graduates had no standard fellowship route into research. This UGC scheme gives them the opportunity to undertake advanced study and research in engineering and technology (and agricultural engineering) leading to a Ph.D., through direct interviews. _Source: UGC; Department of Higher Education._

## Key Benefits
- Fellowship to pursue Ph.D. research in engineering and technology (and agricultural engineering).
- Tenure of 2 years as Junior Research Fellow (JRF).
- On satisfactory evaluation, extended for 3 more years as Senior Research Fellow (SRF) with enhanced emoluments (up to 5 years total).
- If first two years' work is not satisfactory, one improvement year is given (still as JRF); on later satisfactory evaluation, two more years as SRF may follow.
- About 50 slots awarded per year.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for UGC JRF (Engineering & Technology):
- Candidates wishing to pursue research leading to a Ph.D. in engineering and technology.
- M.E., M.Tech. or M.Pharma graduates (since UGC/CSIR do not conduct NET in these fields).
- Selection through direct interviews conducted by the Commission.
- Subjects like geology and geophysics are NOT considered under this scheme.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Selection is via DIRECT INTERVIEW by the UGC (not a NET-type written exam).
- Only about 50 slots are available per year — it is highly competitive.
- Geology and geophysics are explicitly excluded.
- Continuation from JRF (2 yrs) to SRF (3 yrs) depends on a satisfactory expert evaluation of your research work.
- Unsatisfactory work in the first two years gives only a one-year improvement window (still designated JRF).
- It is for the engineering/technology (and agricultural engineering) research stream, leading to a Ph.D.

- Proof of admission/registration for Ph.D. (as applicable).

## Application Process
**Apply:** Respond to the UGC notification for the Junior Research Fellowship in Engineering & Technology with your M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma qualifications and research area.

**Direct interview:** The Commission conducts direct interviews to select candidates (about 50 slots/year).

**Fellowship tenure:** Selected fellows begin as JRF for two years; on satisfactory expert evaluation, they continue as SRF for three more years (up to five years total) toward the Ph.D. Follow UGC guidelines for emoluments and progress reports. _Source: UGC._

## Processing Time
Per the UGC notification/interview schedule; the fellowship then runs up to five years (2 JRF + 3 SRF) subject to evaluations.

## Verification Process
UGC verifies qualifications (M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma) and selects via direct interview; research progress is evaluated by experts before the JRF-to-SRF upgrade.

## Approval Criteria
Approved on selection through the UGC direct interview for eligible engineering/technology research candidates within the ~50 annual slots.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Not an M.E./M.Tech./M.Pharma graduate / not in an eligible field.
- Research area in excluded subjects (geology/geophysics).
- Not selected in the competitive direct interview (limited slots).
- Unsatisfactory research evaluation (affects JRF-to-SRF continuation).

## Renewal Process
Tenure progresses from JRF (2 years) to SRF (3 years) on satisfactory expert evaluation; an additional one-year improvement window applies if early work is unsatisfactory.

## Cancellation Conditions
Discontinued if research work remains unsatisfactory after the improvement period, or on leaving the program/loss of eligibility.

## State-wise Variations
National UGC scheme; not State-specific.

INTRODUCTION
The scheme "Junior Research Fellowship in Engineering & Technology"
by the University Grants Commission (UGC), Department of Higher
Education is meant for candidates who wish to pursue research leading
to a Ph.D. degree in engineering and technology. As of date, the
University Grants Commission (UGC) or the Council of Scientific and
Industrial Research (CSIR) are not conducting National Education
Testing (NET) examinations in these fields. Therefore, M.Pharma and
M.E., M.Tech. students are given this opportunity, through direct
interviews conducted by the Commission. There are 50 slots under this
scheme per year.

OBJECTIVES
The objective of this scheme is to provide an opportunity to research
scholars to undertake advanced study and research in engineering and
technology, and agricultural engineering, leading to Ph.D. Subjects like
geology and geo-physics are not considered under this scheme.
Benefits
NATURE OF ASSISTANCE
The tenure of the fellowship is initially for a period of two years as a
Junior Research Fellowship (JRF).
After the expiry of two years the work of the fellow will be evaluated by
experts and if found satisfactory, his/her tenure will be extended for a
further period of three years with enhanced emoluments as a Senior
Research Fellowship (SRF).
In case the work of the fellow for the first two years is not found
satisfactory, one more year will be given to him/her for improvement, but
during this period he/she will be designated as a Junior Research
Fellow. In such cases work will again be evaluated after three years,
and if the improvement is found, the fellow will get two more years under
the SRF.
Thus, the total period of fellowship (JRF and SRF) is five years, which is
non-extendable. The awardee can avail research fellowship for the full
tenure of the fellowship or up to the date of award of the Ph.D. degree,
whichever is earlier.
