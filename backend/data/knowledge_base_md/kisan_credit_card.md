# Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

## Overview
The Kisan Credit Card gives farmers a revolving, low-interest credit line (effectively 4% with timely repayment) for crops and allied activities, with no collateral up to Rs. 2 lakh.

In plain terms: It works like a farm overdraft: a farmer draws money when needed for seeds, fertiliser or allied activities and repays after harvest, at a heavily subsidised interest rate. Kisan Credit Card (KCC) (KCC) is a central sector credit scheme (Modified interest subvention scheme) of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare / Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. As of 31 December 2024, about Rs. 10.05 lakh crore was outstanding under operative KCCs benefiting 7.72 crore farmers.

**Primary sources for this section:** PIB (KCC press releases); fasalrin.gov.in (Kisan Rin Portal); Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
The Kisan Credit Card gives farmers a revolving, low-interest credit line (effectively 4% with timely repayment) for crops and allied activities, with no collateral up to Rs. 2 lakh.

## Storytelling Version
Instead of going to a moneylender at 24-60% interest, a farmer with a Kisan Credit Card can borrow from a bank at an effective 4% a year if they repay on time. The card works like a flexible credit line linked to the crop cycle: take money when you need it for seeds, fertiliser, fuel or even dairy and fishery work, and repay after you sell your harvest. For loans up to Rs. 2 lakh you do not have to pledge land or gold. PM-KISAN farmers can get a KCC through a short, pre-filled form.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Farmers face a timing gap between spending on inputs and earning from harvest, which historically pushed them toward costly informal loans. KCC was created to give farmers simple, timely, low-cost institutional credit linked to their actual crop needs, bringing them into the formal banking system and protecting them from rural indebtedness. Under the Modified Interest Subvention Scheme the Government subsidises the interest so farmers effectively pay only 4% on prompt repayment. _Source: PIB; Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare; RBI/NABARD._

## Key Benefits
- Effective interest rate of 4% per year on short-term crop loans up to Rs. 5 lakh (7% base, with 3% Prompt Repayment Incentive on timely repayment).
- Collateral-free loans up to Rs. 2 lakh per borrower (raised from Rs. 1.6 lakh, effective 1 January 2025).
- Crop-loan limit under the Modified Interest Subvention Scheme raised from Rs. 3 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh (Union Budget 2025-26).
- Revolving credit valid up to 5 years, with about 10% annual increase in limit, subject to review.
- Covers crop production, post-harvest, household needs, and allied activities (animal husbandry, dairy, fisheries since 2018-19; combined allied limit up to Rs. 3 lakh).
- RuPay KCC debit card for ATM/PoS/UPI use; integrated insurance (PMFBY for crops, PAIS personal accident cover).
- Interest waiver/relief provisions during notified natural calamities.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for KCC:
- All farmers — individual or joint owner-cultivators.
- Tenant farmers, oral lessees and sharecroppers.
- Self-Help Groups (SHGs) or Joint Liability Groups (JLGs) of farmers, including tenants and sharecroppers.
- Farmers engaged in allied activities — animal husbandry, dairy, poultry, fisheries.
- PM-KISAN beneficiaries (eligible for a simplified KCC).

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Aadhaar submission to the bank is mandatory to claim the interest subvention.
- The 4% effective rate applies only on prompt/timely repayment; otherwise the rate rises (7% and above).
- Collateral may be required for limits above Rs. 2 lakh (or above Rs. 3 lakh in tie-up arrangements).
- Loan limit is calculated from the District-Level Technical Committee's scale of finance times area cultivated, plus an allowance for household/post-harvest needs.
- The card must be reviewed annually; limits and validity are subject to renewal.

## Required Documents
- Duly filled application form with passport-size photographs.
- Identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID/Passport/Driving Licence).
- Address proof.
- Proof of landholding certified by revenue authorities (for owner cultivators).
- Cropping pattern (crops grown) with acreage.
- For allied activities: relevant proof (e.g. animal/fishery details).

## Application Process
**Through a bank:** Approach any Commercial Bank, Regional Rural Bank, Small Finance Bank or Cooperative Bank branch, submit the KCC form with land and crop details and KYC documents; the bank assesses the limit and issues the card.

**Online / digital:** Apply via the Kisan Rin Portal (https://www.fasalrin.gov.in) or your bank's digital channel (e.g. PSB/YONO). PM-KISAN beneficiaries get a simplified, pre-filled single-page form.

**Track:** Loan and subvention status can be tracked on the Kisan Rin Portal. _Source: PIB; fasalrin.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Varies by bank; KCC applications are typically processed within about 14 working days under RBI guidance once documents are complete.

## Verification Process
The bank verifies land records, identity and cropping pattern, and computes the credit limit from the official scale of finance. Aadhaar seeding is verified for interest-subvention eligibility.

## Approval Criteria
Approval depends on valid landholding/cultivation proof, acceptable credit history (no default), and the bank's limit assessment based on the scale of finance.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Existing default to any bank or financial institution.
- Aadhaar not submitted/seeded (blocks interest subvention).
- Incomplete land/cropping documents.
- Land not verifiable in revenue records.
- Requested limit exceeds the assessed scale of finance.

## Renewal Process
The KCC is valid up to 5 years and is reviewed annually; the limit typically increases about 10% each year. Renewal is done through the issuing bank.

## Cancellation Conditions
Credit can be withdrawn or the account classified as non-performing on default; interest subvention is lost without timely repayment or Aadhaar seeding.

## State-wise Variations
The core MISS framework is national. Cooperative banks (large in some States) and State scale-of-finance committees mean local limits and crops differ. Some States add their own interest subvention on top.

## Latest Updates
- Collateral-free limit raised from Rs. 1.6 lakh to Rs. 2 lakh per borrower, effective 1 January 2025. _Source: PIB / Lok Sabha reply, 2025._
- MISS crop-loan limit raised from Rs. 3 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh (Union Budget 2025-26).
- Credit limit for fisheries and allied activities raised from Rs. 2 lakh to Rs. 5 lakh in 2025-26.
- Kisan Rin Portal (fasalrin.gov.in) digitises applications and interest-subvention claims.
- As of 31 December 2024: ~Rs. 10.05 lakh crore outstanding across 7.72 crore operative KCCs.

## Application Deadline
Currently Open. KCCs can be applied for any time, but crop-loan limits are tied to seasons — for Kharif, applying around April-May aligns with the sowing cycle.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: What interest rate do I actually pay on a KCC?**

The base rate is 7% per year on short-term crop loans up to Rs. 5 lakh; if you repay on time you get a 3% Prompt Repayment Incentive, bringing the effective rate down to 4%.

**Q: Do I need collateral for a Kisan Credit Card?**

No collateral is needed up to Rs. 2 lakh per borrower (raised from Rs. 1.6 lakh, effective 1 January 2025). Above that, banks may ask for collateral, except up to Rs. 3 lakh in tie-up arrangements.

**Q: Can tenant farmers and sharecroppers get a KCC?**

Yes. Tenant farmers, oral lessees, sharecroppers, and SHGs/JLGs of such farmers are eligible.

**Q: Can I use a KCC for dairy, poultry or fisheries?**

Yes. Since 2018-19 KCC covers allied activities like animal husbandry, dairy, poultry and fisheries, with a combined allied limit up to Rs. 3 lakh.

**Q: How is my KCC limit decided?**

It is based on the District-Level Technical Committee's 'scale of finance' for your crop multiplied by your cultivated area, plus about 10% for household and post-harvest needs.

**Q: I am a PM-KISAN beneficiary. Is getting a KCC easier?**

Yes. PM-KISAN beneficiaries get a simplified, pre-filled single-page KCC application through the linked process.

**Q: What is the Kisan Rin Portal?**

It is the official digital platform (fasalrin.gov.in) for KCC loan applications, status tracking, and processing of interest-subvention and prompt-repayment claims.

**Q: How long is a KCC valid?**

Up to 5 years, with an annual review and roughly 10% annual increase in the sanctioned limit.

**Q: Is KCC a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) is a Central Sector credit scheme (Modified Interest Subvention Scheme) scheme administered by Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare / Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for KCC?**

Applying for Kisan Credit Card (KCC) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://www.myscheme.gov.in/schemes/kcc.

The official website is https://www.myscheme.gov.in/schemes/kcc. The official application portal is https://www.fasalrin.gov.in (Kisan Rin Portal) / through banks. Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

**Q: What is the helpline number for KCC?**

**Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for KCC?**

Aadhaar must be submitted to the bank to claim the interest subvention, and is part of standard KYC for the card.

**Q: Can I apply for KCC at a Common Service Centre (CSC)?**

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

**Q: How will I receive the benefit under KCC?**

KCC is a credit line, not a transfer; funds are drawn via the RuPay KCC card/account, and interest subvention is settled to the bank on your behalf.

**Q: What should I do if my KCC application is rejected?**

Check the rejection reason on the official portal or with the office/bank that processed it, correct the specific issue (commonly an Aadhaar/name/bank mismatch or a missing document), and re-apply or file a grievance through the official grievance channel. Most rejections are due to fixable data errors, not permanent disqualification.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**KCC vs PM-KISAN:** KCC is credit (a loan line); PM-KISAN is free income support. **KCC vs MUDRA:** MUDRA is for non-farm micro-businesses, KCC is for agriculture and allied activities. **KCC vs gold/informal loans:** far cheaper (4% effective vs 24-60%). KCC also bundles PMFBY crop insurance and PAIS accident cover.

## Who Should Apply
Any cultivating farmer — owner, tenant, sharecropper, or one doing dairy/poultry/fishery — who needs affordable seasonal working capital and wants to avoid moneylenders.

## Who Should Not Apply
Those with an active default to a bank, or who only need non-farm business credit (use MUDRA instead).
