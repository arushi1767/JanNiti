# Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

## Overview
PM-KISAN gives every eligible landholding farmer family Rs. 6,000 a year as income support, paid in three Rs. 2,000 instalments straight into their bank account.

In plain terms: A farmer family that owns cultivable land gets Rs. 2,000 deposited every four months — Rs. 6,000 a year — with no middlemen. Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) (PM-KISAN) is a central sector (100% government of india funded) scheme of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. Status as of 2026: Active. The 23rd instalment of approximately Rs. 18,880 crore was released on 20 June 2026 to over 9.44 crore farmers; over Rs. 4.27 lakh crore disbursed since launch.

## One Line Summary
PM-KISAN gives every eligible landholding farmer family Rs. 6,000 a year as income support, paid in three Rs. 2,000 instalments straight into their bank account.

## Storytelling Version
Imagine a small farmer who needs money for seeds and fertiliser before the sowing season but has no cash in hand. PM-KISAN puts Rs. 2,000 into that farmer's own bank account three times a year, automatically, so the family can buy what the crop needs without borrowing from a moneylender. You do not stand in any queue for the money — once you are registered, complete your e-KYC and link your Aadhaar to your bank account, and the Government sends Rs. 6,000 a year directly to you. Think of it as a steady helping hand from the Government for every farming family that owns a piece of land.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Small and marginal farmers often run short of cash exactly when they must buy inputs like seeds, fertiliser and pesticides, pushing many toward high-interest informal loans. The Government launched PM-KISAN in 2019 to give landholding farmer families a predictable, direct cash cushion so they can meet farming and household needs on time, reduce dependence on moneylenders, and stabilise their income. It is one of the world's largest Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) schemes. _Source: Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare; PIB._

## Key Benefits
- Income support of Rs. 6,000 per year per eligible farmer family.
- Paid in 3 equal instalments of Rs. 2,000 every four months.
- Transferred directly to the beneficiary's Aadhaar-seeded bank account via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) — no intermediaries.
- 100% funded by the Government of India.
- Linked benefit: PM-KISAN beneficiaries can get a Kisan Credit Card (KCC) easily through a simplified, pre-filled form.
- Access to the Kisan e-Mitra AI chatbot (in 11 languages) and the PMKISAN mobile app with face-authentication e-KYC.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PM-KISAN:
- All landholding farmer families (husband, wife and minor children) that own cultivable land in their name as per State land records.
- Applicant must be an Indian citizen.
- Aadhaar is mandatory and the bank account must be Aadhaar-seeded.
- Mandatory e-KYC must be completed to receive instalments.
- Originally limited to small/marginal farmers (up to 2 hectares); since 1 June 2019 all landholding farmers qualify regardless of land size, subject to exclusions.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- e-KYC is compulsory — without it instalments are withheld.
- Land must be recorded in the applicant's own name; tenant/leased land alone does not qualify.
- Bank account must be linked (seeded) with Aadhaar or the payment fails.
- The 'family' is defined as husband, wife and minor children — only one Rs. 6,000 benefit per family.
- Income-tax payers in the family are excluded.
- Institutional landholders and families of constitutional post holders, serving/retired government employees (above Group D), professionals (doctors, lawyers, CAs, engineers, architects in practice) and pensioners drawing Rs. 10,000+/month are excluded.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar card (mandatory).
- Land ownership / record-of-rights documents (khasra/khatauni).
- Bank account passbook (Aadhaar-seeded account, with IFSC).
- Aadhaar-linked mobile number (for OTP and e-KYC).
- Citizenship proof such as Voter ID (where asked).

## Application Process
**Online (self-registration):** Visit https://pmkisan.gov.in -> Farmers Corner -> 'New Farmer Registration' -> choose Rural/Urban farmer -> enter Aadhaar, mobile number and State -> verify OTP -> fill personal, bank and land details -> submit. State government verifies in roughly 2-4 weeks.

**e-KYC (mandatory):** Farmers Corner -> 'e-KYC' -> enter Aadhaar -> OTP on Aadhaar-linked mobile -> submit. Face-authentication e-KYC is available in the PMKISAN app; biometric e-KYC can be done at a CSC.

**Offline / assisted:** Apply at the nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) or through the local revenue/agriculture officer.

**Check status:** Farmers Corner -> 'Know Your Status' / 'Beneficiary Status' using Aadhaar, mobile or account number. _Source: pmkisan.gov.in._

## Processing Time
State/UT verification of a new registration typically takes about 2-4 weeks; instalments are then released in the next national payment cycle.

## Verification Process
State/UT governments verify the applicant's land records, Aadhaar, bank seeding and e-KYC. Records are cross-checked to remove ineligible and duplicate beneficiaries before each instalment.

## Approval Criteria
A farmer is approved when land is recorded in their name, Aadhaar and bank seeding and e-KYC are complete, and the family falls under no exclusion category.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- e-KYC not completed.
- Aadhaar not linked / not seeded with the bank account.
- Name mismatch between Aadhaar, bank and land records.
- Land records not updated or not in the applicant's name.
- Beneficiary falls under an exclusion category (income-tax payer, government employee, etc.).
- Incorrect or incomplete bank/IFSC details (status shows 'FTO generated, payment failed').

## Renewal Process
No renewal needed. Once approved, instalments continue automatically as long as eligibility holds and e-KYC stays valid.

## Cancellation Conditions
Benefits stop if the beneficiary is found ineligible, becomes an income-tax payer, gives false information, or fails mandatory e-KYC. Wrongly received amounts are recovered.

## State-wise Variations
The Rs. 6,000 central benefit is uniform nationwide. Several States run their own additional farmer income schemes that top up PM-KISAN (e.g. State 'Kisan' schemes); these are separate and have their own rules.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much money do I get under PM-KISAN and how often?**

Rs. 6,000 per year, paid as three instalments of Rs. 2,000 every four months, directly into your Aadhaar-seeded bank account.

**Q: Why have I not received my PM-KISAN instalment?**

The most common reasons are pending e-KYC, Aadhaar not seeded with your bank account, a name mismatch across Aadhaar/bank/land records, or unverified land records. Check 'Know Your Status' on pmkisan.gov.in and fix the flagged issue.

**Q: How do I complete e-KYC for PM-KISAN?**

Go to pmkisan.gov.in -> Farmers Corner -> e-KYC -> enter Aadhaar -> OTP on your Aadhaar-linked mobile -> submit. Or use face-authentication in the PMKISAN app, or biometric e-KYC at a CSC.

**Q: Can a tenant farmer or someone farming leased land get PM-KISAN?**

No. The land must be recorded in the applicant's own name in State land records. Cultivating only leased/tenanted land does not qualify.

**Q: My land is jointly owned by my family. Can each member apply?**

No. The benefit is one Rs. 6,000 per family (husband, wife and minor children), not per individual.

**Q: How do I check whether my name is in the PM-KISAN beneficiary list?**

On pmkisan.gov.in -> Farmers Corner -> 'Beneficiary List', select State, District, Sub-District, Block and Village, then click 'Get Report'.

**Q: Who is NOT eligible for PM-KISAN?**

Income-tax payers, institutional landholders, families of constitutional post holders, serving/retired government employees above Group D, practising professionals (doctors, lawyers, CAs, engineers, architects) and pensioners drawing Rs. 10,000+ per month are excluded.

**Q: Can a PM-KISAN beneficiary get a Kisan Credit Card?**

Yes. PM-KISAN beneficiaries get a simplified, pre-filled KCC application and can obtain short-term crop loans at concessional interest.

**Q: Is the PM-KISAN amount going to increase to Rs. 8,000 or more?**

Farmer organisations have asked for an increase, but as of 2026 the official amount remains Rs. 6,000 per year. Treat any 'increase' claim without an official notification as unverified.

**Q: Is PM-KISAN a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) is a Central Sector (100% Government of India funded) scheme administered by Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for PM-KISAN?**

Applying for Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://pmkisan.gov.in.

The official website is https://pmkisan.gov.in. The official application portal is https://pmkisan.gov.in (Farmers Corner). Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

Yes. Aadhaar is mandatory for PM-KISAN, your bank account must be Aadhaar-seeded, and e-KYC (Aadhaar-based) is compulsory to receive instalments.

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
Related / complementary schemes: **Kisan Credit Card (KCC)** for crop loans, **PM Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)** for crop insurance, and various **State farmer income schemes** that top up PM-KISAN. PM-KISAN is pure income support (cash); KCC is credit; PMFBY is insurance — farmers commonly use all three together.

## Who Should Apply
Small, marginal and other landholding farmers whose land is recorded in their own name and who are not in an excluded category. Especially valuable for families needing cash for inputs before each season.

## Who Should Not Apply
Income-tax payers, government employees (above Group D), institutional landholders, practising professionals, higher pensioners, and anyone cultivating only leased land with no land in their own name.

## Decision Helper
- **Farmer (owns land):** Apply — this is your core income-support scheme; also get a KCC.
- **Tenant/landless farmer:** Not eligible for PM-KISAN; look at KCC (for tenant farmers/sharecroppers) and State schemes.
- **Woman farmer (land in her name):** Fully eligible — apply in her own name.
- **Government employee / income-tax payer:** Not eligible.
