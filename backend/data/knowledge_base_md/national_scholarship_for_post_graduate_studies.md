# National Scholarship for Post Graduate Studies (NSPG)

## Overview
NSPG gives meritorious students Rs. 15,000 per month (for 10 months a year) to support their first full-time postgraduate degree in India.

In plain terms: A meritorious first-year PG student (under 30) gets Rs. 15,000 a month for 10 months a year to support their master's studies. National Scholarship for Post Graduate Studies (NSPG) (NSPG) is a central merit-based postgraduate scholarship scheme of Ministry of Education. Status as of 2026: Active (annual). Provides Rs. 15,000 per month (for 10 months a year) to meritorious students pursuing their first full-time postgraduate degree.

## One Line Summary
NSPG gives meritorious students Rs. 15,000 per month (for 10 months a year) to support their first full-time postgraduate degree in India.

## Storytelling Version
Pursuing a postgraduate degree can be a financial strain for a meritorious student from a modest background. The National Scholarship for Post Graduate Studies eases that: if you are under 30 and enrolled in the first year of a recognised, full-time master's programme (your first PG degree), you can get Rs. 15,000 a month for 10 months in a year. The aim is to make sure talented students can focus on higher studies without money getting in the way. You apply online on the National Scholarship Portal.

## Why This Scheme Exists
The Ministry of Education set up NSPG to give financial assistance to meritorious students — especially from economically weaker sections — so they can pursue postgraduate studies in India. It supports advanced study, builds a skilled, educated workforce, and reduces the chance of talented students dropping out for financial reasons. _Source: Ministry of Education; National Scholarship Portal._

## Key Benefits
- Scholarship of Rs. 15,000 per month.
- Paid for 10 months in a year.
- Supports the first full-time postgraduate degree.
- Disbursed via DBT to the student's bank account through the National Scholarship Portal.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for NSPG:
- Must be pursuing the FIRST postgraduate degree (those who already completed a PG degree are not eligible).
- Must be enrolled in the FIRST YEAR of the postgraduate program.
- Must be under 30 years old.
- Must be enrolled in a FULL-TIME program.
- Must be pursuing a recognised postgraduate degree program.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Only the FIRST PG degree qualifies — if you already hold a master's degree you are not eligible.
- Only first-year, full-time PG students (not distance/part-time, and not second-year-onwards fresh applicants).
- Age must be under 30.
- Application is online only via the National Scholarship Portal, with e-KYC/OTR (One-Time Registration) using Aadhaar Face Authentication.
- Continuation depends on satisfactory academic performance each year.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar card (for OTR / e-KYC via Face Authentication).
- Admission proof / enrolment in first-year full-time PG.
- Qualifying (UG) mark sheets and proof of age.
- Bank account details (for DBT).
- Income/category certificates if required by the specific component.
- Passport-size photograph.

## Application Process
**One-Time Registration (OTR):** On the National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in) -> 'New Registration', accept the undertaking, verify your mobile via OTP, and complete e-KYC; install the AadhaarFaceRD service and the NSP OTR app and do 'eKYC with FaceAuth' to generate your OTR ID and reference number.

**Apply:** Log in with your OTR ID, fill the scholarship application (select the postgraduate scholarship), upload admission and KYC documents, and submit before the deadline.

**Verification & payment:** The institution and authorities verify the application; the scholarship is paid via DBT. _Source: scholarships.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Processed in the annual NSP cycle after institutional/authority verification; payment via DBT once approved.

## Verification Process
The institution verifies admission on the NSP; the sanctioning authority verifies eligibility (first PG, age, full-time, recognised program) before approval.

## Approval Criteria
Approved for meritorious students under 30 in the first year of a recognised full-time first PG degree, subject to the scheme's selection criteria.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Already holds a postgraduate degree (not the first PG).
- Not a first-year or not a full-time PG student.
- Age 30 or above.
- Program/institution not recognised.
- Incomplete OTR/e-KYC or unverified application.

## Renewal Process
Continues for the course duration subject to satisfactory academic performance; re-validate on the NSP each year as required.

## Cancellation Conditions
Stops on discontinuation, poor academic performance, ineligibility, or false information.

## State-wise Variations
National scheme via the NSP; not State-specific.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much does NSPG pay?**

Rs. 15,000 per month, for 10 months in a year, to support your first full-time postgraduate degree.

**Q: Who is eligible for NSPG?**

A student under 30, enrolled in the first year of a recognised, full-time postgraduate program, pursuing their first PG degree (those who already have a PG degree are not eligible).

**Q: Can I apply if I already have a master's degree?**

No. NSPG supports only the first PG degree; if you already hold a postgraduate degree you are not eligible.

**Q: Is distance/part-time PG covered?**

No. You must be in a full-time program.

**Q: How do I apply?**

Online on the National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in): complete One-Time Registration (OTR) with Aadhaar Face Authentication, then fill and submit the scholarship application with your admission and KYC documents.

**Q: What is OTR and why do I need it?**

OTR (One-Time Registration) creates a unique student ID via Aadhaar e-KYC/Face Authentication, used to apply for scholarships on the NSP.

**Q: How is the money paid?**

Through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to your Aadhaar-seeded bank account.

**Q: Is there an age limit?**

Yes — you must be under 30 years old.

**Q: Is NSPG a central scheme or a state scheme?**

National Scholarship for Post Graduate Studies (NSPG) is a Central merit-based postgraduate scholarship scheme administered by Ministry of Education. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for NSPG?**

Applying for National Scholarship for Post Graduate Studies (NSPG) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://scholarships.gov.in.
