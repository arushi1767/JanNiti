# National Talent Scholarship (Undergraduate) - ICAR

## Overview
The ICAR National Talent Scholarship gives Rs. 3,000 per month to Bachelor's-degree students in Agriculture (and allied subjects) who got admission through the All India Entrance Examination and study outside their home State.

In plain terms: A B.Sc Agriculture student who got in via the All India entrance and studies in another State gets Rs. 3,000 a month for the degree. National Talent Scholarship (Undergraduate) - ICAR (ICAR NTS (UG)) is a central scholarship (agricultural higher education, merit/mobility) scheme of Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. Status as of 2026: Active. Provides Rs. 3,000 per month to UG Agriculture students admitted through AIEE who study in an ICAR-funded Agricultural University outside their home State.

## One Line Summary
The ICAR National Talent Scholarship gives Rs. 3,000 per month to Bachelor's-degree students in Agriculture (and allied subjects) who got admission through the All India Entrance Examination and study outside their home State.

## Storytelling Version
To build national talent in agriculture and encourage students to study across States, ICAR runs the National Talent Scholarship. If you secured admission to a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture or an allied subject through the All India Entrance Examination, and you study in an ICAR-funded Agricultural University outside your home State, you get Rs. 3,000 every month through your degree. It rewards merit and national mobility, helping create well-rounded agricultural professionals.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Under ICAR's scheme 'Strengthening and Development of Higher Agricultural Education in India', the National Talent Scholarship supports meritorious UG students who study agriculture outside their home State, encouraging national integration, merit and quality in agricultural education. _Source: ICAR; Department of Agricultural Research and Education._

## Key Benefits
- Rs. 3,000 per month as scholarship assistance during the Bachelor's degree programme.
- Awarded initially for one year from the date of admission.
- Renewable for the remaining duration of the degree, subject to satisfactory academic performance and conduct.
- Note: No contingency grant is provided under the scheme.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for ICAR NTS (UG):
- Pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture or an allied discipline.
- Secured admission through the All India Entrance Examination (AIEE) conducted for ICAR admissions.
- Enrolled in an Agricultural University located OUTSIDE the student's State of domicile.
- The university must be funded under ICAR's 'Strengthening and Development of Higher Agricultural Education in India' scheme.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- The defining condition is studying OUTSIDE your home State (State of domicile) — students in their home-State university are not eligible.
- Admission must be specifically through the ICAR All India Entrance Examination (AIEE/AIEEA) — not State/other quotas.
- No contingency grant — only the monthly assistance.
- Continuation requires maintaining merit and good conduct, certified by the Head of the College.
- The university must be ICAR-funded under the specific scheme.

## Required Documents
- Proof of admission through the ICAR All India Entrance Examination (AIEE/AIEEA).
- Domicile certificate (to establish study outside the home State).
- Admission/enrolment proof at the ICAR-funded Agricultural University.
- Academic mark sheets.
- Bank account details.
- Aadhaar and passport-size photograph.

## Application Process
**Through ICAR admission:** Qualify the ICAR All India Entrance Examination (AIEEA, conducted by NTA) and take admission to a Bachelor's program in Agriculture/allied at an ICAR-funded Agricultural University outside your home State.

**Scholarship award:** Eligible students admitted via AIEE under the ICAR seat allocation are considered for the National Talent Scholarship as per ICAR norms; the award is for one year and renewed for the rest of the degree on satisfactory performance. Follow the ICAR Education Division/university process for disbursement. _Source: ICAR._

## Processing Time
Linked to the ICAR admission/academic cycle; the award starts from the date of admission and renews annually.

## Verification Process
ICAR/the university verifies AIEE admission, out-of-State status, and academic performance/conduct (certified by the Head of College) for award and renewal.

## Approval Criteria
Approved for AIEE-admitted UG agriculture students studying outside their home State at an ICAR-funded university, subject to merit.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Not admitted through the ICAR AIEE (e.g. State-quota admission).
- Studying within the home State of domicile.
- University not ICAR-funded under the scheme.
- Failure to maintain merit/conduct (affects renewal).

## Renewal Process
Renewed for the remaining degree duration, year to year, subject to satisfactory academic performance and good conduct certified by the Head of the College.

## Cancellation Conditions
Discontinued on poor performance/conduct, leaving the program, or loss of eligibility.

## State-wise Variations
By design the benefit applies only to students studying OUTSIDE their home State; available across ICAR-funded Agricultural Universities nationwide.
