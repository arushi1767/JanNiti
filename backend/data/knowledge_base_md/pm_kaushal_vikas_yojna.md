# Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana - Short Term Training (PMKVY 4.0)

## Overview
PMKVY gives youth free, industry-aligned short-term skill training with certification (and often on-the-job training), so they can get jobs or become self-employed.

In plain terms: You can take a free, government-recognised skill course (with certification) in trades from electronics to AI, and get help finding a job afterwards. Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana - Short Term Training (PMKVY 4.0) (PMKVY) is a central sector skill-development scheme (free short-term training) scheme of Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE). Status as of 2026: Active. PMKVY 4.0 runs to 2026 under the composite Skill India Programme (outlay ~Rs. 8,800 crore); over 1.4 crore trained under PMKVY overall; new AI/drone/green-skill courses added.

**Primary sources for this section:** MSDE / skillindiadigital.gov.in (official); PIB / PMINDIA (Skill India Programme); IBEF; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
PMKVY gives youth free, industry-aligned short-term skill training with certification (and often on-the-job training), so they can get jobs or become self-employed.

## Storytelling Version
If you are a young person who left school or college and wants a real skill that leads to a job, PMKVY trains you free of cost. You pick an industry-aligned course — from electrician, beautician or welder to new-age skills like AI, drones, cybersecurity and green hydrogen — learn at an accredited training centre with on-the-job exposure, and earn a nationally recognised certificate. The scheme also runs Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to certify skills you already have, and helps connect you to employers.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Only a small fraction of India's workforce has formal vocational training, leaving its demographic dividend underused. PMKVY (under the Skill India Mission) was launched in 2015 to provide free, standardised, industry-aligned skilling and certification so youth become employable. PMKVY 4.0 added on-the-job training, flexible/multilingual learning and future-skill courses. _Source: MSDE; PIB; pmindia.gov.in._

## Key Benefits
- Free short-term skill training (STT) aligned to the National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF).
- Nationally recognised certification on assessment.
- On-the-job training (OJT) integrated for real industry exposure.
- Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to certify existing skills (with a small RPL reward in earlier phases).
- 400+ new-age courses (AI, 5G, cybersecurity, green hydrogen, drones) plus traditional trades.
- Training in multiple regional languages; placement/'Recruit-Train-Deploy' linkages and apprenticeship pathways.
- Access via Skill Hubs in IITs, NITs, JNVs, Kendriya Vidyalayas and other institutions.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PMKVY:
- Indian citizen, typically aged 15-45 years (PMKVY 4.0 target group spans 15-59 for RPL/upskilling).
- Unemployed youth, school/college dropouts, or those seeking to up-skill/re-skill.
- Basic eligibility (age/education) depends on the specific job role/course (Qualification Pack).
- Aadhaar for enrolment and certification.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Training is free, but seats are batch- and centre-based — you must enrol in an available batch at an accredited centre/Skill Hub.
- Certification requires passing the assessment by an accredited Assessment Agency.
- Course eligibility (minimum age/education) varies by job role's Qualification Pack.
- Placement is facilitated (Recruit-Train-Deploy) but not guaranteed; outcomes vary by sector and centre.
- Stipend/reward provisions differ by component (e.g. RPL) and phase; verify the current course terms.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar card.
- Bank account details (for any reward/stipend/DBT, where applicable).
- Educational certificates as required by the chosen course.
- Passport-size photograph and a valid mobile number/email.

## Application Process
**Register:** Create an account on the Skill India Digital Hub (skillindiadigital.gov.in) and search for courses/training centres near you, or visit an accredited training centre / Skill Hub.

**Enrol & train:** Join an available batch in your chosen NSQF-aligned course; complete classroom training plus on-the-job training.

**Get certified:** Take the assessment by an accredited agency; on passing you receive a nationally recognised certificate, and placement/apprenticeship support is facilitated. RPL candidates are assessed/certified for existing skills. _Source: skillindiadigital.gov.in; MSDE._

## Processing Time
Short-term courses commonly run for a few weeks to a few months depending on the job role; certification follows the end-of-course assessment.

## Verification Process
Eligibility for a course is checked against its Qualification Pack; skills are validated by an accredited Assessment Agency before certification.

## Approval Criteria
Enrolment is approved on meeting the course's eligibility; certification is 'approved' on passing the standardised assessment.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Not meeting the course's minimum age/education (Qualification Pack) requirement.
- No available batch/seat at the chosen centre.
- Failing the certification assessment (can usually re-attempt).
- Incomplete enrolment documents (Aadhaar, etc.).

## Renewal Process
Not applicable — it is a one-time course/certification; you can take further courses to up-skill or re-skill.

## Cancellation Conditions
Enrolment can lapse on dropping out; certification is withheld if the assessment is not cleared.

## State-wise Variations
National framework via NSDC/SSCs; State Skill Development Missions and State-specific job roles/centres mean availability and trades differ locally.

## Latest Updates
- PMKVY 4.0 (2022-2026) merged with PM-NAPS and JSS into the composite Skill India Programme (outlay ~Rs. 8,800 crore), approved March 2025. _Source: PMINDIA / MSDE._
- 400+ new-age courses (AI, 5G, cybersecurity, green hydrogen, drone tech) added; OJT integrated.
- Training via Skill Hubs in IITs, NITs, JNVs, Kendriya Vidyalayas, etc.; courses in 8+ regional languages.
- Over 1.4 crore trained under PMKVY overall; a next phase (PMKVY 5.0) is under formulation.
- Enrol and find courses on the Skill India Digital Hub (skillindiadigital.gov.in).

## Application Deadline
Currently Open (rolling). Enrolment is batch-based at accredited centres/Skill Hubs — search the Skill India Digital Hub for the next available batch in your area.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: Is PMKVY training really free?**

Yes. Short-term training under PMKVY 4.0 is provided free of cost at accredited training centres/Skill Hubs, with nationally recognised certification on passing the assessment.

**Q: Who can join PMKVY?**

Indian citizens, typically aged 15-45 (up to 59 for RPL/upskilling) — unemployed youth, school/college dropouts, and those wanting to up-skill or re-skill. Exact age/education depends on the course.

**Q: What courses are available?**

Hundreds of NSQF-aligned courses across sectors, including traditional trades (electrician, welder, beautician) and new-age skills like AI, 5G, cybersecurity, green hydrogen and drone technology.

**Q: Will PMKVY get me a job?**

PMKVY facilitates placement through Recruit-Train-Deploy and apprenticeship links, but a job is not guaranteed; outcomes vary by sector, centre and effort.

**Q: What is Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)?**

RPL certifies skills you already have (e.g. from years of informal work) after an assessment, so your experience gets formal recognition.

**Q: How do I enrol?**

Register on the Skill India Digital Hub (skillindiadigital.gov.in), find a course/centre near you, and join an available batch; or visit an accredited training centre/Skill Hub.

**Q: Do I get a certificate?**

Yes — a nationally recognised, NSQF-aligned certificate after you pass the assessment by an accredited Assessment Agency.

**Q: Is there a stipend?**

Some components (like RPL) carried rewards in earlier phases; stipend/reward terms vary by course and phase, so check the current course details.

**Q: Is PMKVY a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana - Short Term Training (PMKVY 4.0) is a Central Sector skill-development scheme (free short-term training) scheme administered by Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE). It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for PMKVY?**

Applying for Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana - Short Term Training (PMKVY 4.0) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://www.skillindiadigital.gov.in.

**Q: Which is the official website for PMKVY?**

The official website is https://www.skillindiadigital.gov.in. The official application portal is https://www.skillindiadigital.gov.in (Skill India Digital Hub). Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

**Q: What is the helpline number for PMKVY?**

**Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for PMKVY?**

Aadhaar is used for enrolment and certification; link a bank account for any applicable reward/stipend via DBT.

**Q: Can I apply for PMKVY at a Common Service Centre (CSC)?**

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

**Q: How will I receive the benefit under PMKVY?**

The benefit is free training and certification; any reward/stipend (where applicable) is paid via DBT to your bank account.

**Q: What should I do if my PMKVY application is rejected?**

Check the rejection reason on the official portal or with the office/bank that processed it, correct the specific issue (commonly an Aadhaar/name/bank mismatch or a missing document), and re-apply or file a grievance through the official grievance channel. Most rejections are due to fixable data errors, not permanent disqualification.

**Q: Is PMKVY still active in 2026?**

Yes. PMKVY 4.0 is active to 2026 under the Skill India Programme, with a next phase under formulation.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**PMKVY vs Skill Loan:** PMKVY gives free short-term training; the Model Skill Loan funds higher-cost/advanced courses with a loan. **PMKVY vs PM-NAPS:** PM-NAPS is apprenticeship-based earn-while-you-learn. All sit under the **Skill India Programme**; PMKVY skilling can lead into formal jobs supported by **PM-VBRY**.

## Who Should Apply
Unemployed youth, school/college dropouts, and workers wanting a recognised skill or to up-skill into better/new-age jobs or self-employment.

## Who Should Not Apply
Those seeking a degree/long professional programme, or who only need financing for a costly course (consider the Skill Loan) rather than free short-term training.

## Decision Helper
- **Dropout/unemployed youth:** Take a free PMKVY course to get job-ready with certification.
- **Already skilled informally:** Get certified via RPL.
- **Want a costly/advanced course:** Use the Model Skill Loan to fund it.
- **Newly employed after training:** PM-VBRY may give a first-job incentive.
