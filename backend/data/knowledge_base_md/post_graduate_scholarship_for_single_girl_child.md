# Post Graduate Indira Gandhi Scholarship for Single Girl Child (PG-IGSSGC)

## Overview
This UGC scholarship gives Rs. 36,200 per year for two years to single girl children pursuing a first-year, full-time, non-professional postgraduate course in a recognised university/college.

In plain terms: A single girl child in her first year of a regular PG (non-professional) course gets Rs. 36,200 a year for the two-year course. Post Graduate Indira Gandhi Scholarship for Single Girl Child (PG-IGSSGC) (PG Indira Gandhi SGC Scholarship) is a central scholarship scheme (single girl child, PG level) scheme of Ministry of Education. Status as of 2026: Active (annual). Provides Rs. 36,200 per year for two years (the full PG course) to about 3,000 single girl children pursuing non-professional PG study.

## One Line Summary
This UGC scholarship gives Rs. 36,200 per year for two years to single girl children pursuing a first-year, full-time, non-professional postgraduate course in a recognised university/college.

## Storytelling Version
When a family's only daughter goes on to postgraduate study, this UGC scholarship supports her. Named after Indira Gandhi, it gives Rs. 36,200 a year — for the full two-year PG course — to single girl children admitted in the first year of a regular, full-time, non-professional master's programme. It is meant to recognise the value of the single girl child, encourage families to support her higher education, and promote small-family norms. About 3,000 girls are selected each year, and the money is paid directly to her bank account.

## Why This Scheme Exists
To promote higher education among single girl children and compensate the direct costs of their education, the UGC (under the Ministry of Education) runs this PG-level scholarship. It supports daughters who are the only girl child in their family, encouraging families to invest in their daughters and supporting a favourable view of the girl child. _Source: UGC; Ministry of Education._

## Key Benefits
- Scholarship of Rs. 36,200 per year.
- Paid for two years, covering the full duration of the PG course (no extension).
- Disbursed via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to the student's bank account (Canara Bank is the nodal bank).
- About 3,000 fresh scholarships awarded each year.
- Can be held along with other scholarships (it is an incentive scheme).

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PG Indira Gandhi SGC Scholarship:
- Must be a single girl child — a girl with no brother; twin/fraternal daughters (with no male sibling) are also eligible.
- Admitted in the FIRST YEAR of a regular, full-time, non-professional postgraduate course in a recognised university or PG college.
- Age not more than 30 years at the time of admission.
- Studying at an eligible institution (UGC Act Section 2(f)/12(B), Section 3 deemed universities receiving government funds, Institutes of National Importance, etc.).
- Indian citizen.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Only NON-PROFESSIONAL PG courses qualify — professional courses like MBA, M.Tech, MBBS, etc. are NOT covered.
- Distance-education / correspondence PG courses are NOT eligible.
- Available only in the FIRST YEAR of the PG course (you must apply as a fresh first-year student).
- Age must not exceed 30 at admission.
- For the second year, a 'Continuation Certificate' must be submitted (often every three months) to keep receiving the scholarship.
- If the student discontinues without informing UGC, she may have to refund the entire amount received; scoring below 55% (50% for PwD) in the first year can lead to termination.
- Application is online only, via the National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in).

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar card.
- Single-girl-child affidavit on Rs. 50 stamp paper, attested by a First-Class Magistrate/SDM/Gazetted Officer.
- Joining/admission certificate from the head of the institution (first year, full-time PG).
- Class/qualifying mark sheets and proof of age.
- Bank account details (for DBT).
- Passport-size photograph.

## Application Process
**Apply online (NSP):** Go to https://scholarships.gov.in -> 'New Registration', read the UGC guidelines, register and get your Application ID/password; log in, choose 'Post-Matric Scholarships' and then 'UGC - Indira Gandhi PG Scholarship for Single Girl Child'.

**Upload documents:** Aadhaar, single-girl-child affidavit, admission/joining certificate, mark sheets and bank details, in the prescribed format.

**Selection & payment:** Shortlisted candidates are published on the UGC website/NSP dashboard; Rs. 36,200/year is paid via DBT. Submit the Continuation Certificate for the second year. _Source: UGC; scholarships.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Processed in the annual NSP cycle after institutional and UGC verification; payment is via DBT once selected.

## Verification Process
The institution verifies admission on the NSP; UGC verifies single-girl-child status, age, course type and eligibility before selection.

## Approval Criteria
Approved for eligible single girl children (under 30) admitted to a first-year, full-time, non-professional PG course at a recognised institution, within the ~3,000 annual quota.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Has a brother / not a single girl child (twins excepted).
- Professional or distance-education course (not eligible).
- Age over 30 at admission, or not a first-year PG student.
- Institution not UGC-recognised / not covered under the scheme.
- Incomplete affidavit/admission certificate or application not verified by the institution.

## Renewal Process
For the second year, submit the 'Continuation Certificate' (typically every three months) in the prescribed format; no separate fresh application, but continued eligibility/marks are required.

## Cancellation Conditions
Terminated for discontinuation, scoring below 55% (50% for PwD) in the first year, misconduct, or false information; the full amount may have to be refunded if the student leaves without informing UGC.

## State-wise Variations
Uniform nationwide via UGC and the National Scholarship Portal; not State-specific.
