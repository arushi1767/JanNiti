# Pradhan Mantri Garib Kalyan Anna Yojana (PMGKAY)

## Overview
PMGKAY gives free foodgrains every month to about 81 crore poor people — 5 kg of rice/wheat per person for Priority Households and 35 kg per family for Antyodaya households — through ration shops, till December 2028.

In plain terms: If you have an NFSA ration card (Priority Household or Antyodaya), you get your monthly foodgrains free of cost at the ration shop. ## One Line Summary
PMGKAY gives free foodgrains every month to about 81 crore poor people — 5 kg of rice/wheat per person for Priority Households and 35 kg per family for Antyodaya households — through ration shops, till December 2028.

## Storytelling Version
For a poor family, a guaranteed supply of free grain each month is the difference between hunger and security. Under PMGKAY, every person in a Priority Household gets 5 kg of rice or wheat free each month, and the poorest Antyodaya families get 35 kg per household — at zero cost. You do not need a new application: if you already have an NFSA ration card, just go to your Fair Price Shop, complete Aadhaar authentication, and collect your grain. With One Nation One Ration Card, you can even collect it anywhere in India.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Launched in March 2020 to protect the poor during the COVID-19 lockdown, PMGKAY became the world's largest food-security programme. From January 2023 it was merged with the National Food Security Act (NFSA) and made completely free, ensuring no eligible family goes hungry. It has been extended to December 2028. _Source: Department of Food and Public Distribution; PIB; Wikipedia summarising government data._

## Key Benefits
- Free foodgrains every month — no payment at the ration shop.
- Priority Households (PHH): 5 kg of rice or wheat per person per month, free.
- Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) households: 35 kg of foodgrains per family per month, free.
- 100% fortified rice (iron, folic acid, Vitamin B12) supplied across the country to improve nutrition.
- One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC): collect your ration from any Fair Price Shop anywhere in India (useful for migrants).
- Mera Ration 2.0 app to check entitlement, withdrawal history and find the nearest FPS.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PMGKAY:
- Must be covered under the National Food Security Act (NFSA), 2013.
- Either an Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) household (poorest of the poor) or a Priority Household (PHH).
- Must hold a valid NFSA ration card.
- Aadhaar-based authentication (e-KYC) at the Fair Price Shop is required.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- There is NO separate PMGKAY application — benefits are automatic for existing NFSA AAY/PHH ration-card holders.
- To get a ration card you must apply under NFSA through your State PDS portal/Tehsildar/CSC (subject to State coverage limits).
- e-KYC / Aadhaar authentication at the FPS is required; un-seeded or unverified cards can face issues.
- Coverage is capped by NFSA limits (up to 75% rural and 50% urban population), so not everyone qualifies even if poor.
- Earlier wider categories (e.g. all Jan Dhan women, migrants) are no longer separately covered — only AAY and PHH under NFSA now.

## Required Documents
- Valid NFSA ration card (AAY or PHH).
- Aadhaar of family members (for e-KYC/authentication).
- For a new ration card (NFSA): Aadhaar of all members, bank passbook, residence proof, applied via State PDS portal/Tehsildar/CSC.

## Application Process
**To receive grain:** No application is needed if you have an NFSA card. Visit your assigned Fair Price Shop (ration shop), give your ration card number/Aadhaar, complete biometric/OTP authentication on the ePoS device, and collect your free monthly quota.

**To get a new NFSA ration card:** Apply through your State's PDS/food department portal, Tehsildar office or CSC with Aadhaar, residence proof and family details; field verification follows. _Source: dfpd.gov.in; nfsa.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Grain is collected monthly at the FPS. A new NFSA ration card typically takes about 15-30 days for State verification and issuance.

## Verification Process
Identity is verified at the FPS via Aadhaar-based ePoS authentication. New ration cards are verified by State food/revenue authorities against NFSA criteria.

## Approval Criteria
Foodgrains are released to any valid AAY/PHH cardholder who passes FPS authentication; ration-card approval is decided by the State under NFSA norms.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- No NFSA ration card / not in the AAY or PHH category.
- Aadhaar not seeded / e-KYC pending at the FPS.
- Card cancelled as duplicate/ineligible during State verification.
- Household above NFSA coverage limits.

## Renewal Process
No renewal of the benefit; it continues monthly while the NFSA card is valid and the scheme runs (currently to December 2028). Cards may need periodic e-KYC.

## Cancellation Conditions
Benefit stops if the ration card is cancelled (ineligibility, duplication, death of all members) or e-KYC is not completed.

## State-wise Variations
Whether rice or wheat is supplied (and the mix) varies by State dietary preference; ration-card application processes and portals are State-specific.

## Latest Updates
- Extended for five years: 1 January 2024 to December 2028, ~Rs. 11.80 lakh crore outlay. _Source: PIB / Cabinet decision._
- Free of cost since 1 January 2023 (integrated with NFSA).
- 100% fortified rice supplied nationwide since March 2024.
- Mera Ration 2.0 app launched (20 Aug 2024) for entitlement/withdrawal tracking and FPS locator.
- About 81.35 crore beneficiaries; Aadhaar-linked ePoS authentication and ONORC nationwide.

## Application Deadline
Ongoing through December 2028. There is no application deadline — eligible NFSA cardholders collect free grain every month at their Fair Price Shop.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
PMGKAY operates within the **NFSA, 2013** framework (it makes the NFSA entitlement free). It complements cash schemes like **PM-KISAN** (income support) — PMGKAY is in-kind food security, not cash. Some States run additional food schemes on top.
