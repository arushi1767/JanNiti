# Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

## Overview
PMJDY gives every adult a zero-balance bank account with a free RuPay debit card, Rs. 2 lakh accident insurance, an overdraft up to Rs. 10,000, and a gateway to DBT, pension and insurance schemes.

In plain terms: It is a free, no-minimum-balance bank account for the unbanked, bundled with a RuPay card, accident cover and a small overdraft. Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) (PMJDY / Jan Dhan) is a central sector national mission for financial inclusion scheme of Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. Over 56-57 crore Jan Dhan accounts opened (around 56% held by women), with deposits crossing roughly Rs. 2.6-3 lakh crore by 2025-26.

**Primary sources for this section:** pmjdy.gov.in (official); PIB (11 years of PMJDY, 2025); pmindia.gov.in; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
PMJDY gives every adult a zero-balance bank account with a free RuPay debit card, Rs. 2 lakh accident insurance, an overdraft up to Rs. 10,000, and a gateway to DBT, pension and insurance schemes.

## Storytelling Version
For millions who never had a bank account, PMJDY opened the door to formal banking. Walk into any bank or Bank Mitra and open a Jan Dhan account with zero balance and no charges. You get a free RuPay debit card with Rs. 2 lakh accidental insurance, and after six months of regular use you can get a small overdraft (up to Rs. 10,000) for emergencies. Best of all, government benefits like PM-KISAN, LPG subsidy and MGNREGA wages come straight into this account — no middlemen.

## Why This Scheme Exists
A large part of India had no bank account, relying on cash and informal lenders and missing out on direct benefit transfers. PMJDY was launched in 2014 as a National Mission for Financial Inclusion to give every household a basic account and access to credit, insurance and pension, and to form the 'JAM' (Jan Dhan-Aadhaar-Mobile) backbone for leak-proof welfare delivery. _Source: pmjdy.gov.in; PIB; pmindia.gov.in._

## Key Benefits
- Zero-balance Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account (BSBDA) with no minimum-balance requirement and no charges.
- Free RuPay debit card.
- Accidental insurance cover of Rs. 2 lakh on RuPay cards for accounts opened after 28 August 2018 (Rs. 1 lakh for older accounts).
- Overdraft facility up to Rs. 10,000 after 6 months of satisfactory operation (first Rs. 2,000 with no conditions); OD age limit 18-65.
- One-time life cover of Rs. 30,000 for eligible accounts opened between 15 Aug 2014 and 31 Jan 2015.
- Interest on deposits; direct gateway to DBT, PMJJBY, PMSBY, APY and MUDRA.
- Last-mile access through Business Correspondents (Bank Mitras).

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PMJDY / Jan Dhan:
- Any Indian citizen aged 18 to 65 can open an account in their own name (minors above 10 can open with a guardian).
- Person should not already have a bank account (the scheme targets the unbanked), though existing account holders can also convert/use BSBDA features.
- Valid KYC (Aadhaar or other Officially Valid Document); 'Small Accounts' can be opened with limited KYC for up to 12 months.
- Overdraft: account active and satisfactorily operated for at least 6 months; one OD per household (women given priority).

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Accident insurance is valid only if the RuPay card was used at least once (financial or non-financial) within 90 days prior to the accident.
- Accident cover is Rs. 2 lakh only for accounts opened after 28 August 2018; older accounts have Rs. 1 lakh.
- Overdraft (up to Rs. 10,000) needs 6 months of active use; only one member per household; age 18-65.
- The Rs. 30,000 life cover was a one-time benefit limited to accounts opened 15 Aug 2014-31 Jan 2015.
- 'Small Accounts' (opened without full KYC) are valid only 12 months (extendable on proof of KYC application) and have transaction limits.
- No transactions for two years can make the account dormant.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar card (or other Officially Valid Document for KYC).
- If no OVD: a 'Small Account' can be opened with a self-attested photograph and signature/thumb impression (limited validity).
- Recent passport-size photograph.
- Filled PMJDY account-opening form (available in Hindi/English).

## Application Process
**Open an account:** Visit any bank branch or Business Correspondent (Bank Mitra), ask for the 'PMJDY Account Opening Form', submit KYC (Aadhaar/OVD) and a photo; many banks do instant e-KYC (OTP/biometric). You receive an account number, passbook and RuPay card.

**Overdraft:** After 6 months of satisfactory operation, request the OD (up to Rs. 10,000) at the branch.

**Link schemes:** Enrol the account in PMJJBY, PMSBY and APY, and seed Aadhaar for DBT. _Source: pmjdy.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Account opening is often same-day with e-KYC. The RuPay card is delivered to the registered address; overdraft is available after 6 months of use.

## Verification Process
The bank verifies KYC (Aadhaar/OVD). For overdraft, the bank checks 6 months of satisfactory account operation and household OD limits.

## Approval Criteria
Account opening is approved for any eligible unbanked adult with valid KYC; OD approval depends on satisfactory operation and household eligibility.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Invalid or incomplete KYC (and Small Account validity lapsed without KYC).
- Overdraft denied if account not operated satisfactorily for 6 months, or another household member already has the OD.
- Accident-insurance claim denied if RuPay card was not used within 90 days before the accident.
- Duplicate account where one already exists (scheme targets the unbanked).

## Renewal Process
No renewal needed for the account. Keep it active (occasional transactions) to avoid dormancy and to retain RuPay accident-insurance eligibility.

## Cancellation Conditions
Account can become dormant after two years of no transactions; insurance lapses if the usage condition is not met. The account itself can be closed on request.

## State-wise Variations
Uniform nationwide through all banks and Bank Mitras; outreach intensity varies by State through saturation campaigns.

## Latest Updates
- Over 56-57 crore accounts opened by 2025-26, ~56% held by women; deposits ~Rs. 2.6-3 lakh crore. _Source: PIB, 2025-26._
- Overdraft limit is up to Rs. 10,000; OD age limit raised to 18-65.
- Accident cover Rs. 2 lakh on RuPay cards for accounts opened after 28 Aug 2018.
- Nationwide saturation campaign (1 Jul-30 Sep 2025) to deepen PMJDY/PMJJBY/PMSBY/APY coverage; KYC re-verification drive for inactive accounts.
- Forms the JAM trinity backbone for Direct Benefit Transfer.

## Application Deadline
Currently Open. There is no deadline — any eligible person can open a Jan Dhan account at any time at a bank or Bank Mitra.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: What do I get with a Jan Dhan account?**

A zero-balance bank account, a free RuPay debit card, Rs. 2 lakh accidental insurance (accounts after 28 Aug 2018), an overdraft up to Rs. 10,000 after 6 months, and a gateway to DBT, insurance and pension schemes.

**Q: Is there any minimum balance or charge?**

No. PMJDY accounts have no minimum-balance requirement and no account-opening or maintenance charge; the basic RuPay card is free.

**Q: How much overdraft can I get and when?**

Up to Rs. 10,000 after 6 months of satisfactory account operation (the first Rs. 2,000 with no conditions). One OD per household, age 18-65, women given priority.

**Q: How much is the accident insurance and how do I keep it active?**

Rs. 2 lakh for accounts opened after 28 August 2018 (Rs. 1 lakh for older accounts). You must use the RuPay card at least once within 90 days before the accident to keep the cover active.

**Q: Can I open a Jan Dhan account without Aadhaar?**

Yes, as a 'Small Account' with a photo and signature/thumb impression, valid for 12 months (extendable on showing you have applied for KYC documents), with transaction limits.

**Q: Can I link other schemes to my Jan Dhan account?**

Yes — Jan Dhan accounts are the base for PMJJBY (life), PMSBY (accident), APY (pension), MUDRA loans and all DBT subsidies.

**Q: Will government subsidies come into this account?**

Yes. With Aadhaar seeding, benefits like PM-KISAN, LPG subsidy, scholarships and MGNREGA wages are credited directly via DBT.

**Q: What if I don't use my account for a long time?**

After two years of no transactions the account can become dormant; make an occasional small transaction to keep it active.

**Q: Is PMJDY / Jan Dhan a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a Central Sector National Mission for Financial Inclusion scheme administered by Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Applying for Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://www.pmjdy.gov.in.

**Q: Which is the official website for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

The official website is https://www.pmjdy.gov.in. The official application portal is Any bank branch / Business Correspondent (Bank Mitra). Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

**Q: What is the helpline number for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

The official helpline / contact for Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is: National Toll-Free 1800-11-0001 / 1800-180-1111. Use only official helplines listed on https://www.pmjdy.gov.in; never share OTPs or bank passwords with callers.

**Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Aadhaar is the easiest KYC and is needed for Aadhaar-seeded DBT; without an OVD you can open a limited 'Small Account' for 12 months.

**Q: Can I apply for PMJDY / Jan Dhan at a Common Service Centre (CSC)?**

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

**Q: How will I receive the benefit under PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Government subsidies and benefits are credited directly to your Aadhaar-seeded Jan Dhan account via DBT.

**Q: What should I do if my PMJDY / Jan Dhan application is rejected?**

Check the rejection reason on the official portal or with the office/bank that processed it, correct the specific issue (commonly an Aadhaar/name/bank mismatch or a missing document), and re-apply or file a grievance through the official grievance channel. Most rejections are due to fixable data errors, not permanent disqualification.

**Q: Is PMJDY / Jan Dhan still active in 2026?**

Yes. PMJDY is active in 2026 with 56-57 crore accounts and ongoing saturation campaigns.

**Q: Can I benefit from PMJDY / Jan Dhan along with other government schemes?**

It depends on the specific scheme rules. Some benefits can be combined with other schemes; others have exclusions. Check the eligibility and hidden-conditions sections, or confirm with the implementing office/bank before assuming you can hold both.

**Q: How can I check my application or beneficiary status for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Use the official portal Any bank branch / Business Correspondent (Bank Mitra) and look for a 'status', 'track application' or 'beneficiary status' option, or ask at the office/bank/institution that processed your application. Keep your application/registration ID handy.

**Q: Can I apply for PMJDY / Jan Dhan offline if I cannot use the internet?**

Yes in most cases — you can get help at the relevant bank branch, government office, or a Common Service Centre (CSC). Carry your Aadhaar and the scheme documents listed in the Required Documents section.

**Q: What documents do I need for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

See the Required Documents section. At minimum keep your Aadhaar, bank passbook (Aadhaar-seeded), a recent photograph, and the scheme-specific proofs ready in clear scanned copies.

**Q: I made a mistake in my PMJDY / Jan Dhan application. How do I correct it?**

Log in to the official portal and edit/resubmit if editing is allowed, or contact the processing office/bank to correct the record. Fixing the specific error (often Aadhaar/name/bank details) and re-submitting usually resolves it.

**Q: How do I file a complaint or grievance about PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Use the official grievance channel for the scheme (grievance portal, helpline, or the processing office). Quote your application/registration ID. Details are in the Official Links section.

**Q: Is my personal data safe, and how do I avoid fraud with PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Apply only through official .gov.in / .nic.in portals or recognised banks/CSCs. The Government never asks for your OTP, bank password or a 'processing fee' to release benefits. Ignore unofficial websites, agents and callers demanding money.

**Q: Can women apply for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Yes — women can open Jan Dhan accounts in their own name and are given priority for the overdraft facility; about 56% of accounts are held by women.

**Q: Is there an age requirement for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Adults 18-65 can open an account in their own name; minors above 10 can open with a guardian. Overdraft is for ages 18-65.

**Q: How long does approval take for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Account opening is often same-day with e-KYC. The RuPay card is delivered to the registered address; overdraft is available after 6 months of use.

**Q: Is there a mobile app or which languages can I use for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Many Government of India services are available via the UMANG app and official scheme apps/portals, often in multiple Indian languages. Check https://www.pmjdy.gov.in for the current app and language options.

**Q: What is the main benefit of PMJDY / Jan Dhan in one line?**

PMJDY gives every adult a zero-balance bank account with a free RuPay debit card, Rs. 2 lakh accident insurance, an overdraft up to Rs. 10,000, and a gateway to DBT, pension and insurance schemes.

**Q: Who should NOT apply for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Those who already have and actively use a full-service bank account and need none of the BSBDA-specific features (though anyone can still use Jan Dhan features).

**Q: Does PMJDY / Jan Dhan require any repayment or premium?**

The account is free. Only the optional overdraft (up to Rs. 10,000) is borrowed money that must be repaid; the account and card themselves cost nothing.

**Q: What happens after my PMJDY / Jan Dhan application is approved?**

Once approved and verified, the benefit (cash transfer, card, loan, insurance cover, scholarship or service as applicable) is activated and, where it is a DBT scheme, credited to your Aadhaar-seeded bank account. Keep your KYC and bank details current to avoid interruptions.

**Q: Where can I find the official information for PMJDY / Jan Dhan?**

Always rely on the official website https://www.pmjdy.gov.in and the application portal Any bank branch / Business Correspondent (Bank Mitra), plus the Government's MyScheme portal (myscheme.gov.in). Avoid blogs and unofficial 'apply here' sites.


## Scheme Comparison Metadata
PMJDY is the foundation account that enables **PMJJBY** (life cover), **PMSBY** (accident cover), **APY** (pension) and **MUDRA** (micro-loans), and is the channel for all **DBT** subsidies (PM-KISAN, LPG, scholarships). It is not a benefit-transfer scheme itself but the gateway to them.

## Who Should Apply
Any unbanked adult, especially in rural/low-income households, who needs a free bank account, a debit card, basic insurance and access to DBT and credit.

## Who Should Not Apply
Those who already have and actively use a full-service bank account and need none of the BSBDA-specific features (though anyone can still use Jan Dhan features).

## Decision Helper
- **Unbanked adult:** Open a Jan Dhan account — it's free and unlocks insurance, pension and DBT.
- **Need emergency micro-credit:** Use the Rs. 10,000 overdraft after 6 months.
- **Want insurance/pension:** Link PMJJBY, PMSBY and APY to the account.
- **Want a business loan:** Use the account to access MUDRA.

Details
A National Mission for Financial Inclusion (NMFI), namely, Pradhan
Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) was launched across the Country in
August, 2014 by the Prime Minister with the idea to ensure that citizens
envisage their financial activities. PMJDY provides universal banking
services for every unbanked household, based on the guiding principles
of banking the unbanked, securing the unsecured, funding the unfunded
and serving unserved and underserved areas. In order to give impetus
to financial inclusion initiatives of the Government, PMJDY scheme was
extended beyond 14.08.2018 with the focus on opening of accounts
shifting from “every household” to “every unbanked adult”.
The scheme has been made more attractive with upward revision in
(i) OD limit from Rs. 5,000/- to Rs. 10,000/- and
(ii) Accidental insurance cover on RuPay card holders from Rs.1 lakh
to Rs.2 lakhs.
PMJDY has been successful in increasing banking penetration and
promoting financial inclusion across the country. The scheme has
ensured access to various financial services like availability of basic
savings bank account, access to need based credit, remittances facility,
insurance and pension to the excluded sections i.e. weaker sections &
low-income groups.
Benefits
Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account (BSBDA)
Any Indian citizen eligible to open a regular bank account can open a
BSBDA. Such an account does not require any minimum balance
maintenance. The account holders can use both bank branches, ATMs
and Banking Correspondents (BCs) for depositing and withdrawing
cash. However, one cannot withdraw cash more than four times a
month.
Small Account/Chota Khata
Under Jan Dhan Yojana, people can open small bank accounts without
presenting legal documents. These accounts would be valid normally for
a period of twelve months. Thereafter, such accounts would be allowed
to a continue for a further period of twelve more months, if the
account-holder provides a document showing that he/she has applied
for any of the Officially Valid Document, within 12 months of opening the
small account.
RuPay Debit Card with inbuilt accident insurance
Under PMJDY, a free Rupay debit card, with inbuilt accident insurance
cover of Rs.2 lakhs (Rs 1 lakh for accounts opened before 28.08.2018),
is issued to all beneficiaries.
Overdraft Facility
Under PMJDY, beneficiaries can avail OD facility upto Rs. 10,000/-.
Business Correspondents (BCs)/Bank Mitras
BCs/Bank Mitras are retail agents engaged by banks for providing
banking services at locations other than a bank branch/ATM. It is mainly
meant for the rural areas, where bank branches are far-flung. BCs/Bank
Mitras connect with the residents and guide them with banking solutions
such as savings accounts, deposits, payments and withdrawals, mini
account statements, etc. Banks with their strong network of BCs/Bank
Mitras are providing hassle free services to the customers in remote /
rural areas.
Eligibility
The applicant should be an Indian National.
If minors above ten years apply, they will require support from their legal
guardians for administering their PMJDY account.
Application Process
Offline
Step 1- Go to the official portal of PMJDY.
Step 2- Under the "e-documents" section, you will find live links for
"Account Opening Form". Applicants can access this in both English and
Hindi languages. Click on the appropriate language option.
Step 3- This will open the form in PDF format. Download it and get a
printout.
Step 4- Fill the form manually with all your bank and personal data,
including bank branch, name of town/village, block/district, Aadhaar
number, profession, annual income, details of Kisan credit card, and
others.
Step 5- Once you fill it out, visit your nearest bank branch and submit it.
While submitting the form, you will need to produce certain documents
along with your Mobile number or email Id to qualify for the Jan Dhan
Yojana.
Documents Required
1. AADHAAR
2. Government ID proofs (Voter Card/PAN Card/Ration card)
3. Permanent Address proof (Passport/Driving License/Electricity
Bill/Telephone Bill/Water Bill)
4. Passport size photograph
5. Filled and Signed PMJDY Account opening form
6. Any other document as notified by the Central Government in
consultation with the Regulator.
