# Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY)

## Overview
PMJJBY gives Rs. 2 lakh life insurance (death due to any cause) to bank/post-office account holders aged 18-50 for a low premium of Rs. 436 per year, auto-debited from the account.

In plain terms: For about Rs. 436 a year, your family gets Rs. 2 lakh if you die from any cause; the premium is auto-debited and the cover renews every year. Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY) (PMJJBY) is a central sector life insurance scheme (one-year renewable term cover) scheme of Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. As on 29 April 2026 cumulative PMJJBY enrolments crossed 27.43 crore and about Rs. 21,512.50 crore had been paid for 10,75,625 claims.

**Primary sources for this section:** jansuraksha.gov.in (official); PIB (Jan Suraksha 11-year release, May 2026); Ministry of Finance / DFS; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
PMJJBY gives Rs. 2 lakh life insurance (death due to any cause) to bank/post-office account holders aged 18-50 for a low premium of Rs. 436 per year, auto-debited from the account.

## Storytelling Version
For less than the cost of two cups of tea a month, PMJJBY makes sure your family gets Rs. 2 lakh if something happens to you. Anyone aged 18-50 with a bank or post office account can join: Rs. 436 is quietly auto-debited once a year, and the Rs. 2 lakh life cover (death due to any cause) protects your family. It is one of three simple 'Jan Suraksha' schemes meant to give every household basic financial safety.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Most low-income families had no life insurance and faced ruin if the earning member died. PMJJBY was launched in 2015 to give affordable, no-frills life cover to the masses, using the Jan Dhan banking network so even the poorest can be protected for a tiny premium. _Source: Ministry of Finance; PIB; jansuraksha.gov.in._

## Key Benefits
- Life cover of Rs. 2 lakh on death due to ANY cause, paid to the nominee.
- Low premium of Rs. 436 per year (auto-debited).
- Pro-rata premium for mid-year enrolment: Rs. 436 (Jun-Aug), Rs. 342 (Sep-Nov), Rs. 228 (Dec-Feb), Rs. 114 (Mar-May).
- One-year cover (1 June-31 May), renewable every year via auto-debit.
- Auto-renewal up to age 55 for those on auto-debit (cannot newly join after 50).
- No medical test required to join.
- Premium eligible for deduction under Section 80C (subject to rules); GST-exempt.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PMJJBY:
- Individuals aged 18 to 50 years (entry); cover continues up to age 55 if premium is paid.
- Must hold a savings bank account or post office savings account.
- Must give consent for auto-debit of the premium.
- Aadhaar should be linked to the participating bank account.
- One person can join through only one account (no double cover).

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- A 30-day lien (waiting) period applies from enrolment — death from natural causes in the first 30 days is not paid (accidental death is covered from day one).
- You cannot newly join after turning 50, though existing members are covered to 55.
- Cover lapses if the account balance is insufficient for the auto-debit on the renewal date.
- Cover ends on closing the bank account or being found enrolled through multiple accounts.
- The claim must be filed by the nominee with the bank/insurer (death certificate etc.); having a valid nominee on record is essential.

## Required Documents
- Savings bank / post office account (for auto-debit).
- Aadhaar (linked to the account).
- Consent-cum-declaration form for auto-debit and enrolment.
- Nominee details.
- For a claim: death certificate and nominee's bank details.

## Application Process
**Enrol:** Through your bank/post office — via net/mobile banking ('Insurance'/'Social Security Schemes' -> PMJJBY), by SMS with many banks, or by submitting the consent-cum-declaration form at the branch. Forms are also available at jansuraksha.gov.in in multiple languages.

**Claim:** The nominee approaches the deceased's bank branch within 30 days with the death certificate, completes the claim form (from the bank or jansuraksha.gov.in), and the claim is settled to the nominee's account. _Source: jansuraksha.gov.in; PIB._

## Processing Time
Enrolment is immediate on auto-debit consent. Claims are settled by the insurer through the bank after document verification (typically within weeks).

## Verification Process
The bank confirms enrolment/premium status; the insurer verifies the death certificate and nominee details before settling the Rs. 2 lakh to the nominee.

## Approval Criteria
Cover is in force once the premium is auto-debited and the 30-day lien (for natural death) has passed; a claim is approved on valid proof of death within the cover period.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Death from natural causes within the 30-day lien period.
- Premium not debited (insufficient balance) / cover lapsed.
- No valid nominee on record / incomplete claim documents.
- Enrolment after age 50 (new) or beyond age 55.
- Multiple enrolments (only one cover honoured).

## Renewal Process
Auto-renews every year on 1 June via auto-debit, as long as the account has sufficient balance and the member is within the age limit (up to 55 on auto-debit).

## Cancellation Conditions
Cover ends on age 55, account closure, insufficient balance at renewal, or member opt-out. It can be re-joined later subject to age and a fresh lien period.

## State-wise Variations
Uniform nationwide; offered through all participating banks/post offices and life insurers across States.

## Latest Updates
- Premium is Rs. 436 per year (with pro-rata slabs for mid-year joining). _Source: PIB, May 2026._
- As on 29 April 2026: 27.43 crore cumulative enrolments; ~Rs. 21,512.50 crore paid for 10,75,625 claims.
- Nationwide saturation campaign (Jul-Sep 2025) pushed enrolments across Gram Panchayats and Urban Local Bodies.
- Strongly promoted alongside Jan Dhan accounts to widen coverage.

## Application Deadline
Currently Open. The cover year runs 1 June to 31 May and renews automatically; you can enrol any time (pro-rata premium applies for mid-year joining).

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much does PMJJBY cost and what do I get?**

Rs. 436 per year for Rs. 2 lakh life cover (death due to any cause), paid to your nominee. The premium is auto-debited from your bank account.

**Q: What is the age limit for PMJJBY?**

You can join between 18 and 50 years of age; cover continues up to age 55 as long as you keep paying the premium. You cannot newly join after 50.

**Q: Is there a waiting period?**

Yes — a 30-day lien period from enrolment. Death due to natural causes in the first 30 days is not covered; accidental death is covered from day one.

**Q: Does PMJJBY cover natural death and illness?**

Yes. Unlike accident-only cover, PMJJBY pays Rs. 2 lakh on death due to any cause — natural death, illness or accident (after the lien period for natural death).

**Q: How does my family claim the Rs. 2 lakh?**

The nominee should visit the deceased's bank branch within 30 days with the death certificate and claim form (available at the bank or jansuraksha.gov.in); the amount is paid to the nominee's account.

**Q: Do I need a medical test to join?**

No medical test or health certificate is required to enrol.

**Q: What if my account doesn't have enough balance on renewal day?**

The auto-debit fails and the cover lapses. Keep at least the premium amount in the account before the 1 June renewal date.

**Q: Can I have PMJJBY through more than one bank account?**

No. Even if enrolled through several accounts, only one Rs. 2 lakh cover is honoured; pay the premium through just one account.

**Q: Is PMJJBY a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY) is a Central Sector life insurance scheme (one-year renewable term cover) scheme administered by Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**PMJJBY vs PMSBY:** PMJJBY is life insurance (death by any cause, Rs. 436/yr); PMSBY is accident insurance (Rs. 20/yr). **PMJJBY vs APY:** APY is pension. Together these three 'Jan Suraksha' schemes give life cover, accident cover and pension — many people take all three.

## Who Should Apply
Any earning adult aged 18-50 with a bank account, especially a family's main breadwinner, who wants affordable life cover for their dependants.

## Who Should Not Apply
People over 50 (new joiners), those without a bank account, or those who already have adequate life insurance and don't want this top-up.

## Decision Helper
- **Family breadwinner (18-50):** Enrol — Rs. 2 lakh cover for Rs. 436/year is very cheap protection.
- **Want accident cover too:** Add PMSBY (Rs. 20/year).
- **Want a pension:** Add Atal Pension Yojana.
- **Over 50:** Cannot newly join PMJJBY; look at other term plans.
