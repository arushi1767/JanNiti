# Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY)

## Overview
MUDRA gives collateral-free business loans up to Rs. 20 lakh to non-corporate, non-farm micro and small enterprises, in four stages — Shishu, Kishore, Tarun and Tarun Plus.

In plain terms: If you run or want to start a small non-farm business, you can get a collateral-free loan up to Rs. 20 lakh through banks/NBFCs under MUDRA. Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) (MUDRA / PMMY) is a central sector micro-credit scheme (collateral-free business loans) scheme of Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. In FY25 (to 31 Mar 2025) about 4.79 crore loans worth ~Rs. 5.02 lakh crore were sanctioned; the limit was raised to Rs. 20 lakh under the new 'Tarun Plus' category from 24 October 2024.

**Primary sources for this section:** mudra.org.in (official); PIB (Tarun Plus / Rs. 20 lakh); Ministry of Finance / DFS; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
MUDRA gives collateral-free business loans up to Rs. 20 lakh to non-corporate, non-farm micro and small enterprises, in four stages — Shishu, Kishore, Tarun and Tarun Plus.

## Storytelling Version
A tailor, a tea-stall owner, a small manufacturer or a shopkeeper often cannot get a bank loan because they have no collateral. MUDRA solves this: it lets banks, NBFCs and microfinance institutions give collateral-free loans to small non-farm businesses — Rs. 50,000 to start (Shishu), up to Rs. 5 lakh to grow (Kishore), up to Rs. 10 lakh to expand (Tarun), and now up to Rs. 20 lakh (Tarun Plus) for those who have already repaid a Tarun loan. There is even a MUDRA RuPay card for working capital. Nearly 70% of MUDRA borrowers are women.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Millions of tiny businesses — the backbone of the economy — were 'unfunded', shut out of formal credit for want of collateral. MUDRA ('Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency') was launched in 2015 to 'fund the unfunded', providing collateral-free institutional credit and refinance so small entrepreneurs can start and grow. Loans are guaranteed under the Credit Guarantee Fund for Micro Units (CGFMU). _Source: mudra.org.in; PIB; Ministry of Finance._

## Key Benefits
- Collateral-free loans up to Rs. 20 lakh for non-corporate, non-farm micro/small enterprises.
- Four categories: Shishu (up to Rs. 50,000), Kishore (Rs. 50,001-5 lakh), Tarun (Rs. 5-10 lakh), and Tarun Plus (Rs. 10-20 lakh, for those who repaid a Tarun loan).
- No collateral required (covered by CGFMU guarantee); no fixed government interest rate — set by the lender per RBI norms.
- MUDRA RuPay card for flexible working-capital/overdraft access.
- 2% interest subvention on prompt repayment of Shishu loans (as notified).
- Loans for manufacturing, trading, services, and activities allied to agriculture (dairy, poultry, beekeeping); repayment up to about 7 years.
- Apply online via Jan Samarth / Udyamimitra portals or any lending institution.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for MUDRA / PMMY:
- Any Indian citizen (typically 18-65) running or planning a non-corporate, non-farm income-generating business.
- Business in manufacturing, trading, services, or allied-to-agriculture activities (not crop farming itself).
- Individuals, proprietorships, partnership firms, and companies are eligible.
- Applicant should not be a defaulter to any bank/financial institution and should have an acceptable credit track record.
- Tarun Plus (Rs. 10-20 lakh): only for borrowers who have successfully repaid a previous Tarun loan.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- MUDRA is a refinance agency — it does NOT lend directly; you borrow from a bank/NBFC/MFI.
- There is no single government interest rate; each lender sets it per RBI guidelines (commonly ~8.85%-16%+), so rates and documentation vary by lender.
- Tarun Plus (up to Rs. 20 lakh) is only for those who already repaid a Tarun loan.
- Collateral-free status relies on the CGFMU guarantee; lenders may still assess creditworthiness and viability (project report for larger loans).
- Crop farming is excluded (use KCC); only allied activities like dairy/poultry qualify.
- Some lenders may ask for ITR/financials for Kishore/Tarun loans.

## Required Documents
- Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Driving Licence, Passport).
- Address proof (utility bill, Aadhaar, Passport).
- Business proof / registration (Udyam registration, trade licence) as applicable.
- Passport-size photographs.
- Bank statements / financials and a project report or business plan (for Kishore/Tarun/Tarun Plus).
- Quotation of machinery/items to be purchased (if applicable).

## Application Process
**Choose category** (Shishu/Kishore/Tarun/Tarun Plus) based on your need.

**Apply:** Visit any Member Lending Institution (bank/RRB/SFB/NBFC/MFI) with the form and documents, OR apply online through the Jan Samarth portal (jansamarth.in) or Udyamimitra (udyamimitra.in); PSBloansin59minutes is available for some banks.

**Sanction:** The lender appraises the proposal and, if approved, sanctions the loan (and may issue a MUDRA card). Approval typically takes about 7-10 working days when documents are complete. _Source: mudra.org.in; jansamarth.in._

## Processing Time
Typically about 7-10 working days at a bank branch once documents are in order; online applications via Jan Samarth may route faster.

## Verification Process
The lender verifies KYC, business existence/plan, credit history (CIBIL) and repayment capacity; Shishu loans have lighter checks than Kishore/Tarun.

## Approval Criteria
Approval depends on a viable income-generating business, clean credit record (no default), and the lender's appraisal; Tarun Plus additionally requires a repaid Tarun loan.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Existing default / poor credit history.
- Business is corporate/farm-based or not income-generating (ineligible activity).
- Weak or missing project report/business plan (for larger loans).
- Incomplete KYC/business documents.
- Applying for Tarun Plus without having repaid a Tarun loan.

## Renewal Process
Working-capital limits (MUDRA card) are reviewed periodically by the lender; term loans run to maturity. Borrowers can move up categories (e.g. Tarun to Tarun Plus) on good repayment.

## Cancellation Conditions
The facility can be recalled/classified as NPA on default; interest subvention (Shishu) is lost without prompt repayment.

## State-wise Variations
National scheme; available through all lenders. Some States/banks add interest concessions (e.g. for women entrepreneurs).

## Latest Updates
- Loan limit raised from Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 20 lakh via the new 'Tarun Plus' category, effective 24 October 2024 (Union Budget 2024-25). _Source: PIB / Ministry of Finance._
- CGFMU guarantee extended to cover Tarun Plus loans (collateral-free up to Rs. 20 lakh).
- FY25: ~4.79 crore loans sanctioned worth ~Rs. 5.02 lakh crore; ~52 crore loans and ~Rs. 33.65 lakh crore since 2015.
- Nearly 68-70% of MUDRA accounts belong to women.
- Apply digitally via Jan Samarth (jansamarth.in) and Udyamimitra (udyamimitra.in).

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much loan can I get under MUDRA?**

Up to Rs. 20 lakh, collateral-free: Shishu up to Rs. 50,000, Kishore Rs. 50,001-5 lakh, Tarun Rs. 5-10 lakh, and Tarun Plus Rs. 10-20 lakh (for those who repaid a Tarun loan).

**Q: Is collateral required for a MUDRA loan?**

No. MUDRA loans (including Tarun Plus up to Rs. 20 lakh) are collateral-free, backed by the Credit Guarantee Fund for Micro Units (CGFMU).

**Q: What can I use a MUDRA loan for?**

Any non-corporate, non-farm income-generating business — manufacturing, trading, services, or activities allied to agriculture (dairy, poultry, beekeeping). Crop farming itself is not covered (use KCC).

**Q: What is the interest rate on a MUDRA loan?**

There is no fixed government rate; each lender sets it per RBI norms (commonly around 8.85%-16%+ depending on profile). Shishu loans often have a 2% interest subvention on prompt repayment.

**Q: Who is the Tarun Plus category for?**

Tarun Plus (Rs. 10-20 lakh) is only for entrepreneurs who have already taken and successfully repaid a Tarun loan.

**Q: Does MUDRA give loans directly?**

No. MUDRA is a refinance agency; you borrow from a bank, RRB, Small Finance Bank, NBFC or MFI.

**Q: How do I apply online for a MUDRA loan?**

Through the Jan Samarth portal (jansamarth.in) or Udyamimitra (udyamimitra.in), or visit any lending institution with your KYC and business documents.

**Q: What is a MUDRA card?**

A RuPay-based card linked to your MUDRA loan that gives flexible working-capital/overdraft access at ATMs, PoS and through Business Correspondents.

**Q: Is MUDRA / PMMY a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) is a Central Sector micro-credit scheme (collateral-free business loans) scheme administered by Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for MUDRA / PMMY?**

Applying for Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://www.mudra.org.in.

**Q: Which is the official website for MUDRA / PMMY?**

The official website is https://www.mudra.org.in. The official application portal is https://www.jansamarth.in / https://www.udyamimitra.in / through banks. Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

**Q: What is the helpline number for MUDRA / PMMY?**

The official helpline / contact for Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) is: MUDRA Toll-Free 1800-180-1111 / 1800-11-0001. Use only official helplines listed on https://www.mudra.org.in; never share OTPs or bank passwords with callers.

**Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for MUDRA / PMMY?**

Aadhaar is part of standard KYC for the loan; keep it linked to your bank account.

**Q: Can I apply for MUDRA / PMMY at a Common Service Centre (CSC)?**

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

**Q: How will I receive the benefit under MUDRA / PMMY?**

MUDRA is a loan, not a transfer; funds are disbursed to your business/bank account or accessed via the MUDRA RuPay card.

**Q: What should I do if my MUDRA / PMMY application is rejected?**

Check the rejection reason on the official portal or with the office/bank that processed it, correct the specific issue (commonly an Aadhaar/name/bank mismatch or a missing document), and re-apply or file a grievance through the official grievance channel. Most rejections are due to fixable data errors, not permanent disqualification.

**Q: Is MUDRA / PMMY still active in 2026?**

Yes. MUDRA is active in 2026 with the Rs. 20 lakh Tarun Plus category in force.

**Q: Can I benefit from MUDRA / PMMY along with other government schemes?**

It depends on the specific scheme rules. Some benefits can be combined with other schemes; others have exclusions. Check the eligibility and hidden-conditions sections, or confirm with the implementing office/bank before assuming you can hold both.

Apply only through official .gov.in / .nic.in portals or recognised banks/CSCs. The Government never asks for your OTP, bank password or a 'processing fee' to release benefits. Ignore unofficial websites, agents and callers demanding money.

**Q: What happens after my MUDRA / PMMY application is approved?**

Once approved and verified, the benefit (cash transfer, card, loan, insurance cover, scholarship or service as applicable) is activated and, where it is a DBT scheme, credited to your Aadhaar-seeded bank account. Keep your KYC and bank details current to avoid interruptions.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**MUDRA vs Stand-Up India:** MUDRA is up to Rs. 20 lakh for any micro/small non-farm business; Stand-Up India is Rs. 10 lakh-1 crore specifically for SC/ST and women greenfield enterprises. **MUDRA vs KCC:** KCC is for agriculture; MUDRA is for non-farm business. **MUDRA vs PM-VBRY:** PM-VBRY is an employment incentive, not a loan.

## Who Should Apply
Small and micro entrepreneurs — shopkeepers, artisans, service providers, small manufacturers, dairy/poultry units — who need collateral-free credit to start or grow.

## Who Should Not Apply
Crop farmers (use KCC), large/corporate businesses, defaulters, and those wanting larger SC/ST-women greenfield funding (consider Stand-Up India).
