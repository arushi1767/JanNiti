# Pradhan Mantri Viksit Bharat Rozgar Yojana (PM-VBRY)

## Overview
PM-VBRY (the ELI scheme) gives first-time formal employees a benefit of up to Rs. 15,000 and gives employers incentives for creating new EPFO jobs, to boost formal employment.

In plain terms: First-time EPFO employees get up to Rs. 15,000 (in two parts), and employers get incentives for each additional new employee they hire and retain. Pradhan Mantri Viksit Bharat Rozgar Yojana (PM-VBRY) (PM-VBRY / ELI Scheme) is a central sector employment-linked incentive (ELI) scheme of Ministry of Labour & Employment. Status as of 2026: Active. Launched as the Employment-Linked Incentive (ELI) scheme; registration window 1 Aug 2025-31 Jul 2027, with a central outlay of about Rs. 1 lakh crore and ~3.5 crore expected beneficiaries.

## One Line Summary
PM-VBRY (the ELI scheme) gives first-time formal employees a benefit of up to Rs. 15,000 and gives employers incentives for creating new EPFO jobs, to boost formal employment.

## Storytelling Version
Getting your first formal job — one with Provident Fund and benefits — is a big step, and PM-VBRY rewards it. If you join an EPFO-covered job for the first time, you can receive up to Rs. 15,000 as a one-time incentive, paid in two parts (the second after you complete a financial-literacy course and stay employed). At the same time, the scheme pays employers an incentive for every new employee they add and keep for at least six months, so companies are encouraged to create more formal jobs.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Announced in the Union Budget 2024-25 under the Prime Minister's Package for Employment and Skilling, PM-VBRY (the ELI scheme) was launched to accelerate job creation, formalise the workforce, and support first-time workers, aligned with the Viksit Bharat@2047 vision. It is run by the Ministry of Labour & Employment through EPFO. _Source: pmvbry.epfindia.gov.in; PIB; Ministry of Labour & Employment._

## Key Benefits
- Part A (first-time employees): one-time incentive of up to Rs. 15,000, paid in two instalments — the second after about 12 months of continued EPFO contribution and completing a financial-literacy course.
- Part B (employers): incentives for each additional new employee hired beyond the baseline and retained at least 6 months, credited to the employer's PAN-linked account.
- Encourages formal-sector jobs with Provident Fund and social security.
- First-timer's benefit credited directly to the employee's Aadhaar-seeded bank account (DBT).
- Manufacturing sector gets extended/longer employer incentives.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PM-VBRY / ELI Scheme:
- Part A (employee): a first-time EPFO member (never an EPFO/exempted-trust member before 1 Aug 2025), joining during 1 Aug 2025-31 Jul 2027.
- Employee's gross monthly wage at joining must be below Rs. 1,00,000 (first-timer incentive context references wages up to Rs. 1 lakh; the scheme targets new formal workers).
- Employee must have an Aadhaar-authenticated UAN generated via Face Authentication on the UMANG app, and Aadhaar linked to the bank account.
- Part B (employer): EPFO-registered establishment creating net additional jobs over its baseline (existing establishments deemed registered; new ones register; minimum additional employees thresholds apply by size).
- Both employee and employer contributions must be received in EPFO/exempted trust for at least 6 months for the incentive.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- You must be a genuine FIRST-TIME EPFO member — prior EPFO membership disqualifies you from the first-timer benefit.
- The UAN must be Aadhaar-authenticated via Face Authentication on UMANG; otherwise the employer incentive is not admissible.
- At least 6 months of continuous EPFO contribution is needed before the first instalment; the second needs ~12 months plus a financial-literacy course.
- Employers must update PAN/GSTIN/PAN-linked bank details and meet the baseline-employees condition; jobs created after the window closes don't earn incentives.
- Establishments under investigation or with pending EPFO legal cases are not eligible for employer incentives.
- There is no separate stand-alone application portal beyond EPFO processes — eligibility flows from EPFO records/ECR filings.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar (for UAN authentication and bank seeding).
- Aadhaar-authenticated UAN (Universal Account Number) generated via Face Authentication on UMANG.
- PAN and PAN-linked, Aadhaar-seeded bank account.
- Employment details with an EPFO-registered employer (EPF contribution record).
- Employer: EPFO registration/code, PAN, GSTIN, and ECR (Electronic Challan-cum-Return) filings.

## Application Process
**Employees:** Get a formal job with an EPFO-registered employer; activate your UAN on the EPFO Member portal and complete Aadhaar Face-Authentication on the UMANG app; link Aadhaar to your bank account. Eligibility and the incentive flow automatically from EPFO records once you complete the required months of contribution (and the financial-literacy course for the second instalment).

**Employers:** Existing EPFO establishments are deemed registered (update PAN/GSTIN/bank details); new establishments register with EPFO and file monthly ECRs showing net new employment. Incentives are credited based on EPFO records. Refer to pmvbry.epfindia.gov.in. _Source: EPFO._

## Processing Time
First-timer's first instalment after ~6 months of EPFO contribution; second after ~12 months plus the financial-literacy course. Employer incentives after 6 months' retention of new hires.

## Verification Process
EPFO verifies first-time membership, UAN Aadhaar Face-Authentication, contribution continuity, and (for employers) net additional employment via ECR filings.

## Approval Criteria
The incentive is admissible when the employee is a verified first-time EPFO member with an Aadhaar-authenticated UAN and the required contribution period (employer: verified net new jobs and retention).

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Prior EPFO/exempted-trust membership (not a true first-timer).
- UAN not Aadhaar-authenticated via Face Authentication.
- Required 6/12-month contribution not completed; financial-literacy course not done (for 2nd instalment).
- Employer below baseline / under EPFO investigation / details not updated.
- Wages outside the prescribed band or job created after the registration window.

## Renewal Process
Not applicable — it is a time-bound incentive tied to the 1 Aug 2025-31 Jul 2027 window and the employee's first formal job.

## Cancellation Conditions
Incentives are withheld/recovered if eligibility conditions (continuous contribution, genuine first-timer/new-job status) are not met or are found false.

## State-wise Variations
National scheme run through EPFO; no State-specific benefit variation, though it applies wherever EPFO-covered establishments operate.

Up to Rs. 15,000, paid in two instalments — the first after about 6 months of EPFO contribution and the second after about 12 months plus completing a financial-literacy course.

Someone who was never a member of EPFO or an exempted PF trust before 1 August 2025, joining an EPFO-covered job during 1 Aug 2025-31 Jul 2027.

Incentives for each additional new employee hired beyond their baseline and retained for at least 6 months, credited to the employer's PAN-linked account; manufacturing gets longer cover.

The scheme provides incentives to the employer for two years in

For the manufacturing sector, the incentive to the employer will continue

For additional employees with an Employees' Provident Fund wage less

In respect of employees getting Employees' Provident Fund wages

For additional employees with an Employees' Provident Fund wage

additional employment per month.
The benefits will be provided to all eligible establishments on a
six-monthly lump-sum basis after the filing of six completed months'
Electronic Challan-cum-Returns.
The incentive to the establishment shall be provided as a Direct Benefit
Transfer to the Permanent Account Number-linked bank account of the
establishment.
