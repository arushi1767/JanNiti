# Skill Loan Scheme (Model Skill Loan Scheme)

## Overview
The Skill Loan Scheme gives collateral-free loans (now up to Rs. 7.5 lakh) to students taking skill-development courses, so cost is not a barrier to advanced skilling.

In plain terms: If a skilling course is too expensive to pay upfront, you can get a collateral-free skill loan (up to Rs. 7.5 lakh) backed by a government guarantee. Skill Loan Scheme (Model Skill Loan Scheme) (Skill Loan) is a central sector education/skill credit scheme (collateral-free) scheme of Ministry of Skill Development & Entrepreneurship (MSDE). Status as of 2026: Active. The revamped Model Skill Loan Scheme raised the maximum loan from Rs. 1.5 lakh to Rs. 7.5 lakh (Budget 2024-25), with a 75% credit guarantee and wider lenders/courses; aims to benefit ~25,000 students/year.

**Primary sources for this section:** MSDE (official); PIB (revamped Model Skill Loan); Department of Financial Services / jansamarth.in; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
The Skill Loan Scheme gives collateral-free loans (now up to Rs. 7.5 lakh) to students taking skill-development courses, so cost is not a barrier to advanced skilling.

## Storytelling Version
Some valuable skill courses — in advanced or in-demand trades — cost more than a student can pay upfront. The Model Skill Loan Scheme lets you borrow up to Rs. 7.5 lakh, without any collateral or guarantor, to pay for an eligible skilling course at an ITI, polytechnic, or an NSDC/Sector Skill Council training partner. The loan is backed by a government-promoted guarantee fund (75% cover), so banks, NBFCs and small finance banks can lend to deserving students who would otherwise be left out.

## Why This Scheme Exists
The original 2015 skill loan (max Rs. 1.5 lakh) saw low uptake because the limit was too small for rising course fees. The revamped Model Skill Loan Scheme (Budget 2024-25) raised the limit to Rs. 7.5 lakh, added NBFCs/MFIs/SFBs as lenders, and broadened eligible courses (including non-NSQF courses on the Skill India Digital Hub), so finance is no longer a barrier to advanced, in-demand skilling. _Source: MSDE; PIB; Department of Financial Services._

## Key Benefits
- Collateral-free, guarantor-free loan up to Rs. 7.5 lakh (raised from Rs. 1.5 lakh) for skill-development courses.
- 75% credit guarantee against default (government-promoted fund via NCGTC), enabling wider lending.
- Wider lenders: banks plus NBFCs, NBFC-MFIs and Small Finance Banks.
- Broader course coverage: NSQF-aligned courses and non-NSQF courses onboarded on the Skill India Digital Hub.
- Moratorium (no repayment) during the course plus 6-12 months after; flexible repayment tenure.
- No margin money for smaller loans; reasonable interest (often around base rate + ~1.5%).
- Aims to benefit about 25,000 students per year.

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for Skill Loan:
- Indian national who has secured admission in an eligible skill-development course.
- Course offered by ITIs, polytechnics, schools recognised by central/state boards, colleges affiliated to recognised universities, or training partners affiliated to NSDC/Sector Skill Councils/State Skill Missions.
- Course should be NSQF-aligned or onboarded on the Skill India Digital Hub.
- No minimum-income or specific academic cut-off prescribed centrally (lender may assess).

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- The loan is for skilling courses — not for general higher-education degrees (that is the separate Education Loan / Vidya Lakshmi route).
- Collateral-free status depends on the NCGTC 75% guarantee; the lender still appraises the application.
- Loan amount and tenure depend on the course duration/fees (the Rs. 7.5 lakh is a maximum).
- Repayment starts after a moratorium (course period plus 6-12 months); interest may accrue during the moratorium per lender terms.
- The course/institution must be on the eligible list (NSQF or Skill India Digital Hub).

## Required Documents
- Admission proof/letter for the eligible skill course.
- Identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID/Passport).
- Address proof.
- Academic certificates (as required).
- Bank account details and passport-size photographs.
- Course fee structure/details from the institution.

## Application Process
**Secure admission** in an eligible NSQF/Skill India Digital Hub course at an ITI, polytechnic, or NSDC/SSC training partner.

**Apply:** Through a participating bank/NBFC/SFB branch, or online via the Jan Samarth portal (jansamarth.in) under the education/skill-loan category; submit admission and KYC documents.

**Sanction & disbursal:** The lender appraises and sanctions the collateral-free loan (backed by the NCGTC guarantee); fees are disbursed to the institution. Repayment begins after the moratorium. _Source: jansamarth.in; MSDE._

## Processing Time
Varies by lender; typically a couple of weeks once admission and documents are in order.

## Verification Process
The lender verifies admission, course eligibility (NSQF/Skill India Digital Hub), KYC and basic repayment outlook; the guarantee cover applies to eligible loans.

## Approval Criteria
Approval depends on valid admission in an eligible course, complete KYC, and the lender's appraisal (the guarantee removes the collateral requirement).

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Course/institution not eligible (not NSQF-aligned and not on the Skill India Digital Hub).
- No valid admission/admission proof.
- Incomplete KYC or poor credit indicators.
- Requested amount exceeds the Rs. 7.5 lakh cap or the course's reasonable cost.

## Renewal Process
Not applicable — a one-time course loan; further courses can be financed separately.

## Cancellation Conditions
Facility can be recalled/classified as NPA on default; the NCGTC guarantee covers the lender for eligible defaults.

## State-wise Variations
National scheme; available through participating lenders. State Skill Missions' approved courses also qualify.

## Latest Updates
- Maximum loan raised from Rs. 1.5 lakh to Rs. 7.5 lakh under the revamped Model Skill Loan Scheme (Budget 2024-25). _Source: PIB / MSDE._
- 75% credit guarantee; NBFCs, NBFC-MFIs and Small Finance Banks added as lenders.
- Coverage widened to non-NSQF courses onboarded on the Skill India Digital Hub.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much can I borrow under the Skill Loan Scheme?**

Up to Rs. 7.5 lakh (raised from Rs. 1.5 lakh in 2024), collateral-free, for an eligible skill-development course.

**Q: Do I need collateral or a guarantor?**

No. The loan is collateral-free and guarantor-free, backed by a 75% credit guarantee from a government-promoted fund (NCGTC).

**Q: Which courses qualify?**

Skill courses at ITIs, polytechnics, recognised schools/colleges, or NSDC/Sector Skill Council training partners — NSQF-aligned courses, and non-NSQF courses onboarded on the Skill India Digital Hub.

**Q: When do I start repaying?**

After a moratorium covering the course period plus 6-12 months; tenure is flexible based on the loan amount.

**Q: Is this the same as an education loan for a degree?**

No. This is specifically for skill-development courses. General higher-education degrees use the regular Education Loan (e.g. via Vidya Lakshmi).

**Q: Where do I apply?**

At a participating bank/NBFC/Small Finance Bank, or online through the Jan Samarth portal (jansamarth.in), after securing admission.

**Q: What is the interest rate?**

Set by the lender (often around the base rate + ~1.5%); no fixed government rate. Check with your chosen lender.

**Q: Who added more lenders to this scheme?**

The 2024 revamp expanded eligible lenders beyond IBA-member banks to include NBFCs, NBFC-MFIs and Small Finance Banks.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**Skill Loan vs PMKVY:** PMKVY gives free short-term training; the Skill Loan finances costlier/advanced courses via credit. **Skill Loan vs Education Loan:** the Education Loan (Vidya Lakshmi) funds degree programmes; the Skill Loan funds skill courses. Both are collateral-free up to their limits.

## Who Should Apply
Students who got admission to a valuable but costly skill course (NSQF or Skill India Digital Hub) and need finance without collateral.

## Who Should Not Apply
Those whose course is free under PMKVY (no loan needed), those pursuing a general degree (use the Education Loan), or courses not on the eligible list.

## Decision Helper
- **Costly eligible skill course:** Take a collateral-free Skill Loan (up to Rs. 7.5 lakh).
- **Free short-term course available:** Use PMKVY instead — no loan needed.
- **Pursuing a degree:** Use the Education Loan (Vidya Lakshmi).
- **Course not NSQF/Skill India Digital Hub:** Check eligibility before applying.
