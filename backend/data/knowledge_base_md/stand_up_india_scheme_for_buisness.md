# Stand-Up India

## Overview
Stand-Up India gives bank loans of Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore to Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe and women entrepreneurs to set up a new (greenfield) business.

In plain terms: Every bank branch funds at least one SC/ST and one woman entrepreneur with a Rs. 10 lakh-1 crore composite loan for a first-time (greenfield) enterprise. Stand-Up India (Stand-Up India) is a central sector entrepreneurship/credit scheme (SC/ST & women) scheme of Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. Around 2 lakh-plus entrepreneurs (about 80% women) have been financed; the scheme has been extended (through FY 2025-26 and beyond per government decisions).

**Primary sources for this section:** standupmitra.in (official); Department of Financial Services; PIB; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
Stand-Up India gives bank loans of Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore to Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe and women entrepreneurs to set up a new (greenfield) business.

## Storytelling Version
Starting a real business needs more money than a micro-loan — but SC/ST and women entrepreneurs often struggle to get it. Stand-Up India requires every bank branch in India to finance at least one Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe entrepreneur and at least one woman entrepreneur with a loan of Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore for a brand-new business in manufacturing, services, trading or agri-allied activities. The loan combines term loan and working capital, and the Stand-Up Mitra portal helps with the project report, bank liaison and mentoring.

## Why This Scheme Exists
SC, ST and women entrepreneurs face structural barriers — limited collateral, networks and credit access. Stand-Up India was launched in 2016 to push institutional credit to these under-represented groups for greenfield enterprises, backed by a credit guarantee (CGFSI) and handholding support, to promote inclusive entrepreneurship. _Source: standupmitra.in; Department of Financial Services; PIB._

## Key Benefits
- Composite bank loan of Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore (term loan + working capital) for a greenfield enterprise.
- Covers up to 85% of project cost (borrower brings minimum 10% own contribution; margin can be met via convergence with other schemes, capped so the loan covers ~75-85%).
- Mandate: at least one SC/ST and one woman borrower financed per bank branch.
- Credit guarantee through the Credit Guarantee Fund for Stand-Up India (CGFSI) — reduces/avoids third-party collateral.
- RuPay debit card for the loan account; working capital up to Rs. 10 lakh via overdraft, above that via cash credit.
- Repayment up to 7 years with a moratorium up to 18 months.
- Handholding via Stand-Up Mitra portal (project report, training, mentoring, bank linkage).

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for Stand-Up India:
- SC/ST and/or women entrepreneurs above 18 years of age.
- Loan must be for a greenfield (first-time) venture in manufacturing, services, trading, or activities allied to agriculture.
- For non-individual entities, at least 51% shareholding and controlling stake must be held by SC/ST and/or women entrepreneurs.
- Borrower must not be in default to any bank/financial institution.
- Borrower contributes at least 10% of project cost as own funds.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Strictly GREENFIELD — only a first-time venture; expansion of an existing business is NOT eligible.
- One loan per eligible borrower/family for setting up the unit.
- Caste certificate must be from the proper authority (Tehsildar/SDM); informal certificates are rejected.
- Borrower must bring a minimum 10% own contribution; banks won't disburse without it.
- A vague project report is a common cause of rejection — a solid, viable plan is essential.
- No direct cash subsidy; it is a bank loan (interest applies), with credit guarantee in place of collateral.

## Required Documents
- Filled application form with passport-size photographs.
- Identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID/Passport/Driving Licence).
- Residence proof.
- Proof of SC/ST category (caste certificate from competent authority) and/or proof of being a woman applicant.
- Business address proof and project report / business plan.
- For non-individuals: partnership deed / MoA & AoA; relevant financials/ITR where available.

## Application Process
**Register/check eligibility:** Visit https://www.standupmitra.in, register with location, category (SC/ST/woman) and proposed business; you are classified as a 'Ready Borrower' or 'Trainee Borrower' and the portal routes you to banks and handholding agencies.

**Prepare:** Get your project report (template/handholding help available) and KYC/business documents ready, including the 10% own contribution.

**Apply:** Submit the loan application at a Scheduled Commercial Bank branch (or via the portal / Lead District Manager); the bank appraises and sanctions. Sanction can take about 30-60 days for the larger loan size. _Source: standupmitra.in._

## Processing Time
Larger loan size means appraisal typically takes about 30-60 days (longer than MUDRA), including a pre-sanction site visit.

## Verification Process
The bank verifies category (caste/gender), KYC, project viability and credit history; a pre-sanction site visit is common. The portal supports handholding and readiness assessment.

## Approval Criteria
Approval depends on eligibility (SC/ST/woman, greenfield), a viable project report, clean credit record, the 10% own contribution, and bank appraisal.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Not a greenfield venture (existing business / expansion).
- Applicant not SC/ST/woman, or non-individual entity without 51% SC/ST/woman control.
- Existing default / poor CIBIL record.
- Weak or unrealistic project report.
- Own contribution (10%) not arranged; invalid caste certificate.

## Renewal Process
Working-capital limits are reviewed periodically by the bank; the term loan runs to maturity (up to 7 years with up to 18 months moratorium).

## Cancellation Conditions
The facility can be recalled/classified as NPA on default; the CGFSI guarantee covers eligible defaults for the lender.

## State-wise Variations
National mandate across all bank branches; some States/UTs add their own margin-money or subsidy convergence on top.

## Latest Updates
- Around 2 lakh-plus entrepreneurs financed since launch; about 80% of beneficiaries are women.
- Continued/extended through FY 2025-26 (and per subsequent government decisions). _Source: DFS / PIB._
- Apply and get handholding via the Stand-Up Mitra portal (standupmitra.in); CGFSI provides collateral-free guarantee cover.
- Effective interest typically the bank's base rate (MCLR) + up to 3% + tenor premium (~10-12% in FY 2025-26).

## Application Deadline
Currently Open. There is no deadline — eligible SC/ST/women entrepreneurs can apply any time at a bank branch or via standupmitra.in.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much loan can I get under Stand-Up India?**

A composite loan (term loan + working capital) of Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore for setting up a new (greenfield) enterprise.

**Q: Who is eligible for Stand-Up India?**

SC/ST and/or women entrepreneurs above 18, setting up a first-time (greenfield) business in manufacturing, services, trading or agri-allied activities. For companies/partnerships, 51% control must be with SC/ST and/or women.

**Q: Is this for a new business or can I expand an existing one?**

Only a greenfield (first-time) venture qualifies. Expansion of an existing business is not eligible.

**Q: Do I need collateral?**

Stand-Up India provides a credit guarantee (CGFSI) so third-party collateral is generally not required; the loan is secured by the project assets and the guarantee.

**Q: How much money do I have to bring myself?**

At least 10% of the project cost as your own contribution; the scheme can fund up to about 85% (margin can be met via convergence with other eligible schemes).

**Q: What is the repayment period?**

Up to 7 years, with a moratorium (grace period) of up to 18 months.

**Q: How do I apply?**

Register on standupmitra.in (you'll be marked Ready or Trainee Borrower), prepare your project report and documents, then apply at a Scheduled Commercial Bank branch or through the portal/Lead District Manager.

**Q: How is Stand-Up India different from MUDRA?**

MUDRA funds any micro/small non-farm business up to Rs. 20 lakh; Stand-Up India funds Rs. 10 lakh-1 crore specifically for SC/ST and women greenfield enterprises.

**Q: Is Stand-Up India a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Stand-Up India is a Central Sector entrepreneurship/credit scheme (SC/ST & women) scheme administered by Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

**Q: Is there any fee to apply for Stand-Up India?**

Applying for Stand-Up India through the official portal, a bank branch, or a Common Service Centre (CSC) is free. Beware of agents or websites demanding payment to register you — registration is free of cost. Source: https://www.standupmitra.in.

**Q: Which is the official website for Stand-Up India?**

The official website is https://www.standupmitra.in. The official application portal is https://www.standupmitra.in / nearest bank branch. Always verify the URL ends in .gov.in or .nic.in before entering any personal details.

**Q: What is the helpline number for Stand-Up India?**

The official helpline / contact for Stand-Up India is: Stand-Up Mitra Helpline 1800-180-1111. Use only official helplines listed on https://www.standupmitra.in; never share OTPs or bank passwords with callers.

**Q: Is Aadhaar mandatory for Stand-Up India?**

Aadhaar is part of KYC; a valid caste certificate (for SC/ST) from the competent authority is also required.

**Q: Can I apply for Stand-Up India at a Common Service Centre (CSC)?**

Yes, in most cases you can apply at a nearby Common Service Centre (CSC) if you cannot apply online yourself. Carry your Aadhaar, bank passbook, and the scheme-specific documents.

**Q: How will I receive the benefit under Stand-Up India?**

Stand-Up India is a bank loan, not a transfer; funds are disbursed to the business account and accessed via cash credit/RuPay card.

**Q: What should I do if my Stand-Up India application is rejected?**

Check the rejection reason on the official portal or with the office/bank that processed it, correct the specific issue (commonly an Aadhaar/name/bank mismatch or a missing document), and re-apply or file a grievance through the official grievance channel. Most rejections are due to fixable data errors, not permanent disqualification.

**Q: Is Stand-Up India still active in 2026?**
