# Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY)

## Overview
PMSBY gives Rs. 2 lakh accident cover (death or total disability) and Rs. 1 lakh for partial disability to bank/post-office account holders aged 18-70 for just Rs. 20 per year.

In plain terms: For Rs. 20 a year, you get Rs. 2 lakh if an accident causes death or full disability, and Rs. 1 lakh for partial permanent disability. Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY) (PMSBY) is a central sector accident insurance scheme (one-year renewable) scheme of Ministry of Finance. Status as of 2026: Active. Cumulative enrolments crossed about 56.15 crore as of 7 January 2026; nearly Rs. 3,660 crore had been settled for over 1.84 lakh claims.

**Primary sources for this section:** jansuraksha.gov.in (official); PIB / Wikipedia (premium & enrolment data, 2026); Ministry of Finance / DFS; MyScheme.

## One Line Summary
PMSBY gives Rs. 2 lakh accident cover (death or total disability) and Rs. 1 lakh for partial disability to bank/post-office account holders aged 18-70 for just Rs. 20 per year.

## Storytelling Version
Accidents can take away a family's earning member or their ability to work in an instant. For just Rs. 20 a year — less than the cost of a snack — PMSBY covers anyone aged 18-70 with a bank account: Rs. 2 lakh for accidental death or total permanent disability, and Rs. 1 lakh for partial permanent disability. The premium is auto-debited once a year, so a tiny amount buys real peace of mind.

## Why This Scheme Exists
Accidental death or disability can devastate a poor family that has no insurance. PMSBY was launched in 2015 to provide ultra-cheap accident cover to everyone with a bank account, using the Jan Dhan network to reach the unorganised and rural population. _Source: Ministry of Finance; PIB; jansuraksha.gov.in._

## Key Benefits
- Rs. 2 lakh for accidental death or total/irrecoverable permanent disability.
- Rs. 1 lakh for partial permanent disability (e.g. loss of one limb or eye).
- Very low premium of Rs. 20 per year, auto-debited.
- One-year cover (1 June-31 May), renewable annually.
- GST-exempt; can be held alongside PMJJBY and APY.
- Available to NRIs with an eligible Indian bank account (claims paid in INR to an Indian account).

## Eligibility Criteria
Who can apply for PMSBY:
- Individuals aged 18 to 70 years (Indian resident or NRI with eligible account).
- Must hold a savings bank or post office savings account.
- Must give consent for annual auto-debit of the premium.
- Aadhaar should be linked to the participating account.
- Each holder of a joint account can enrol separately.

## Hidden Conditions
These document-backed conditions are easy to miss and are common reasons benefits get delayed or stopped:
- Covers only ACCIDENTAL death/disability — natural death, suicide, intentional self-injury and criminal acts are NOT covered.
- Cover lapses if the account balance is insufficient for the auto-debit on the renewal date.
- Cover ends at age 70, on account closure, or on multiple enrolments (only one honoured).
- For accidental death claims, documents like the death certificate and FIR/post-mortem (as applicable) are needed; partial-disability claims need a disability certificate.
- Claim should be intimated to the bank promptly (within about 30 days of the incident).

## Required Documents
- Savings bank / post office account (for auto-debit).
- Aadhaar (linked to the account).
- Consent-cum-declaration form for auto-debit and enrolment.
- Nominee details.
- For a claim: FIR/death certificate (accidental death) or disability certificate (disability), and nominee/claimant bank details.

## Application Process
**Enrol:** Through your bank/post office — net/mobile banking ('Social Security Schemes' -> PMSBY), by SMS with many banks, or by submitting the consent-cum-declaration form. Forms are available at jansuraksha.gov.in.

**Claim:** The insured (disability) or nominee (death) approaches the bank branch within about 30 days with the FIR/death certificate or disability certificate and the claim form; the amount is paid via the bank. _Source: jansuraksha.gov.in._

## Processing Time
Enrolment is immediate on auto-debit consent. Accident claims are processed by the insurer through the bank after document verification.

## Verification Process
The bank confirms active cover; the insurer verifies the accident (FIR/medical/disability certificate) before settling Rs. 2 lakh / Rs. 1 lakh as applicable.

## Approval Criteria
Cover is active once the Rs. 20 premium is auto-debited; a claim is approved on proof that death/disability resulted from an accident within the cover period.

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Cause is not an accident (natural death, illness, suicide, self-injury, criminal act).
- Premium not debited / cover lapsed.
- Claim not intimated in time / incomplete documents (FIR, disability certificate).
- Age above 70 / account closed.
- Multiple enrolments (only one cover honoured).

## Renewal Process
Auto-renews each year on 1 June via auto-debit, subject to sufficient balance and age limit (up to 70).

## Cancellation Conditions
Cover ends at age 70, on account closure, insufficient balance at renewal, or opt-out; can be re-joined subject to eligibility.

## State-wise Variations
Uniform nationwide through participating banks/post offices and general insurers.

## Latest Updates
- Premium is Rs. 20 per year for Rs. 2 lakh accident cover. _Source: PIB / jansuraksha.gov.in, 2026._
- Cumulative enrolments ~56.15 crore as of 7 January 2026; ~Rs. 3,660 crore settled for over 1.84 lakh claims.
- Push under the Jul-Sep 2025 nationwide saturation campaign alongside PMJJBY and APY.

## Application Deadline
Currently Open. The cover year runs 1 June to 31 May and renews automatically; you can enrol any time and the Rs. 20 premium is debited for the cover year.

## Generated FAQs
**Q: How much does PMSBY cost and what does it cover?**

Rs. 20 per year for Rs. 2 lakh on accidental death or total permanent disability, and Rs. 1 lakh for partial permanent disability. The premium is auto-debited yearly.

**Q: What is the age limit for PMSBY?**

Individuals aged 18 to 70 with an eligible savings account can enrol; cover ends at age 70.

**Q: Does PMSBY cover natural death or illness?**

No. PMSBY covers accidents only. Natural death, illness, suicide, self-injury and criminal acts are not covered. For death by any cause, see PMJJBY.

**Q: What does partial disability cover?**

Rs. 1 lakh for partial permanent disability, such as loss of one limb or sight in one eye, due to an accident.

**Q: How does my family claim under PMSBY?**

The nominee (for death) or the insured (for disability) approaches the bank within about 30 days with the FIR/death certificate or disability certificate and the claim form.

**Q: Can I take both PMSBY and PMJJBY?**

Yes. Many people take both — Rs. 20 for accident cover (PMSBY) and Rs. 436 for life cover (PMJJBY) — for combined protection.

**Q: Can NRIs enrol in PMSBY?**

Yes, NRIs with an eligible Indian savings account can enrol; claims are paid in Indian Rupees to an Indian bank account.

**Q: What happens if my balance is too low on renewal day?**

The auto-debit fails and your accident cover lapses. Keep at least Rs. 20 in the account before 1 June each year.

**Q: Is PMSBY a central scheme or a state scheme?**

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY) is a Central Sector accident insurance scheme (one-year renewable) scheme administered by Ministry of Finance. It is fully funded and run by the Government of India and is available across all States and Union Territories, though some operational details are handled by State governments and banks.

## Scheme Comparison Metadata
**PMSBY vs PMJJBY:** PMSBY is accident-only cover (Rs. 20/yr); PMJJBY is life cover for death by any cause (Rs. 436/yr). **With APY (pension)** they form the three 'Jan Suraksha' schemes. PMSBY complements, not replaces, life insurance.

## Who Should Apply
Anyone aged 18-70 with a bank account — especially workers, drivers, and earners in risky jobs — wanting extremely cheap accident protection.

## Who Should Not Apply
People over 70, those without a bank account, or those who specifically need life cover for natural death (use PMJJBY) rather than accident cover.

## Decision Helper
- **Any working adult (18-70):** Enrol — Rs. 2 lakh accident cover for Rs. 20/year is almost free protection.
- **Want cover for any cause of death:** Add PMJJBY.
- **Want a pension:** Add Atal Pension Yojana.
- **Over 70:** Not eligible for PMSBY.
